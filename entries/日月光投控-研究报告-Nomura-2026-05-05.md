Vanguard   5347.TWO 5347 TT
Global Markets Research

Interposer is a better business than we thought
Reduced spending and more solid customer commitment 
to drive faster breakeven; upgrade to Buy from Neutral
Milder VSMC ramp-up dilution might unlock EPS upside
Vanguard (VIS) guided 2Q26E wafer shipments to grow 11-13% q-q and blended ASP to 
increase 2-4% q-q (Fig. 2 ; mostly due to pricing uplift), implying c.15% q-q wafer revenue 
growth (in USD terms). This is in line with our preview and underpinned by pricing tailwinds 
and power management (PM) strength holding the utilization rate (UTR) above 85% (report ). 
Management indicated that customers would consign tools to its new 12” fab (VSMC), and 
VSMC would add interposers to the production portfolio (on top of analog/mixed-signal), which 
would together possibly lead to a faster ramp-up. This echoes our view that the site could 
absorb the 65nm silicon interposer/local silicon interconnect (LSI) production outsourced by 
TSMC (2330 TT, Buy) on top of some mature-node demand moving out from TSMC by as 
early as 2027-28F (report ). VIS plans to reduce both VSMC’s capex budget (from USD7.8bn 
to USD6.7bn), due to tool consignments, and VSMC’s installed capacity target by 2029E (from 
55kwpm to 44kwpm), due to more cleanroom space needed for interposer production. We 
believe VIS' depreciation curve would still peak out in 2029-30F. In our view, a more concrete 
UTR planning might drive the fab toward our faster breakeven scenario by simulation (report ), 
but more importantly, the reduced spending (and therefore, lower fixed charges) could unlock 
the capped EPS upside along with better GM fall-through from interposer/LSI outsourcing 
given more favorable pricing, for a milder ramp-up impact vs prior guidance of “high single-digit 
GM dilution”. This should alleviate our concerns and support a more positive stance and 
hence, we upgrade our rating to Buy from Neutral. We raise our 2026F/27F EPS by 14%/28% 
factoring in higher ASP and GM. Our new TP is TWD178 (TWD140 previously) based on 30x 
(unchanged) 2027F EPS of TWD5.94, which is at the high end of VIS’s historical trading band, 
discounting potential analog/mixed-signal and interposer/LSI opportunities flowing out of 
TSMC. VIS currently trades at 27x 2026F P/E.
Other highlights from the earnings call
(1) Outlook: While peers estimate about mid-teens revenue growth for 2026E, Vanguard 
expects to outperform its peers and look for 85-90% UTR going forward as it has some 
unfulfilled demand. Meanwhile, it sees some customers seeking long-term capacity supports 
as some leading foundries are consolidating their 8” fab capacity. (2) Demand for VSMC: 
While VSMC enables customers’ access to supply chain resilience or geographical 
diversification, it also fulfills the needs for more digital controller contents and higher power in 
certain PMIC and analog chips.

Company profile
Vanguard International Semiconductor Corporation is a semiconductor foundry service provider that specializes 8-inch wafer fabrication founded in 
1994. Its product lines could roughly be divided into display driver ICs and power management platforms.
Valuation Methodology
Our TP of TWD178.00 is based on 30x 2027F P/E. Our target P/E multiple of 30x is at the high end of VIS's valuation. Our benchmark index is 
TWSE Index.
Risks that may impede the achievement of the target price
Major upside risks: (1) larger-than-expected price hike magnitude for 8" foundries; (2) stronger-than-expected demand increase in automotive; (3) 
faster-than-expected progress in ramping up incremental fab capacity; and (4) faster-than-expected outsourcing from global IDMs. Major downside 
risks: (1) shortage of 8" fab supply accelerates customers' migration to 12"; (2) oversupply after major 8" foundries' capacity expansion; and (3) 
weaker-than-expected end-product sell-through.
ESG
VIS founded a corporate sustailability committee in 2012 to set regulations and goals for ESG activities Major aspects include governance and 
innovation, green manufacturing, responsible supply chain, friendly workplace and common good. VIS' core ESG mission of "pursuing corporate 
growth through sustainable thinking" could be evidenced by the pay hike in January 2021 after VIS consequently generated robust profits.
