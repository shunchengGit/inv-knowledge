Mar-26 Performance: Intense Product 
Launches & Home Appliance Focus
China Mar online sales grew by 106% YoY on a low base (20% below 2H25 sales 
levels)
We track Pop Mart's China online sales (Tmall/Taobao + Douyin) using Moojing e-
commerce data. In Mar-26, sales showed 106% YoY growth, representing a 5% 
increase MoM. However, comparing Feb&Mar sales to the 2H25 average reveals a 
~20% decline, suggesting potential sales challenges as the base becomes higher 
from 2Q onwards. For 1Q26, Pop Mart's e-commerce growth is estimated at 86% 
YoY, consistent with the 85% YoY growth observed in 4Q25.
Pop Mart is scheduled to release its 1Q26 operational updates in May. Based on 
recent discussions with investors, the market anticipates 80-100% YoY growth 
for the China market across all channels in 1Q26. For overseas sales, market 
expectations point towards a continuous QoQ decline compared to 4Q25, though 
the exact magnitude remains uncertain. In our base case, YoY growth for the 
overseas market is projected to remain at 100% (equivalent to a 10% QoQ decline). 
Should the QoQ decline prove more severe than this base case, it would be 
considered a negative for the full-year forecast.
Pop Mart's Intense 2Q26 Product Pipeline and April Home Appliance 
Introduction
Entering 2Q26, Pop Mart is set to maintain its aggressive new product launch 
schedule as the key strategy to stimulate sales. However, despite the increased 
product releases, we observe signs of "fashion fatigue" among collectors. By 
prioritizing sales volume over perceived scarcity, this strategy has inadvertently 
raised the bar for repurchase among Pop Mart's dedicated collectors. Thus, we 
believe that while an intense product pipeline may initially boost demand, its impact 
will likely diminish later.
Recent product launches have been met with mixed to negative feedback in 
global social media discussions. The "Monster x Sanrio" series, launched on 
March 12th, received relatively weak feedback, primarily attributed to a perceived 
lack of design innovation, with the secondary price in China dropping sharply after 
the debut (now sold at 40% discount). Similarly, the "Monster x FIFA" series, 
launched on April 2nd, has also received comparatively weak feedback, possibly 
due to a limited overlap between "The Monster" target audience and football fans.

China secondary market trends & our recent downgrade
Secondary market prices are a crucial indicator of an IP's popularity and its supply-
demand dynamics. Labubu-related products continue to experience cooling 
demand, likely due to the diminished scarcity of existing collections following 
significant sales volumes in 2025 and 2026 CNY. We believe "Twinkle Twinkle" is 
currently the sole Pop Mart IP considered "scarce" in China. However, its 
secondary market price has begun trading at a discount too, signaling early signs 
of "fashion fatigue," similar to the trend observed with Labubu in 3Q25. "Skull 
Panda," despite high market expectations, has not yet generated similarly strong 
feedback.
We hold a different view on Pop Mart's business model compared to the market, 
which views it as a "platform company" capable of continuously generating new 
sales through multiple blockbuster IPs and new products. Instead, we argue that 
Labubu serves as the critical "hook" for the company, attracting customers to 
stores and driving stronger sales for other IP series and increased units purchased 
per transaction due to the long queues and perceived sense of "premium" & 
"scarcity". Without Labubu's strong appeal, the brand awareness and sales 
traction of other Pop Mart IPs would likely be significantly weaker, particularly in 
overseas markets. 
We downgraded to Sell as we think the 4Q25 deceleration of Pop Mart has raised 
concerns for the 2026 outlook (please refer to our recent downgrade report). As 
collectible IP is a cyclical business, we believe "The Monster," its most popular IP, 
may have already peaked, and there is no other blockbuster IP currently that can 
generate similar global demand. Thus, this fading overseas traction may pressure 
Pop Mart's 2026 outlook, and it is too early to identify the turning point.

Investment Thesis
Outlook
Established in 2010, Pop Mart operates as a designer toy company based in China, 
globally distinguished for popularizing the "blind box" collectibles model. By strate-
gically leveraging proprietary and licensed Intellectual Properties (IPs), such as the 
Monster or Labubu, the company has successfully fostered a passionate interna-
tional collector base, thereby integrating art, design, and a distinctive purchasing 
experience to cultivate substantial market engagement. Labubu experienced 
exceptional performance in global markets, which has played a critical role to Pop 
Mart's success in 2024-2025; however, recent weakening trends of Labubu sug-
gest potential uncertainty for Pop Mart's 2026 sales forecasts. We assign a SELL 
rating after a decelerating trend in 4Q25 after the company reported 2025 annual 
result. Although we commend management's demonstrated strong IP operating 
capabilities in driving the international expansion of China's pop-toy industry, this 
factor does not, at present, alleviate our cautious outlook.
Valuation
We use the discounted cash flow (DCF) method to value Pop Mart. Our DCF 
assumptions include: 1.7% risk-free rate, 1.15 beta, a 6% market risk premium and 
a WACC of 7.9%.  We employ the P/E method as a cross check. Our DCF-based tar-
get price implies a 15x 2026E P/E.
Risks
Downside Risks: (1) Faster than expected existing IP sales momentum globally. (2) 
A new blockbuster IP introduced sooner than anticipated. (3) strong preogress of 
Labubu movie & other innovative product types.
