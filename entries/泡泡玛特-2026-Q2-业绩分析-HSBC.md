Earnings cuts likely to extend as Labubu-driven demand normalises: We expect 
Pop Mart to report 1Q26 revenue of RMB9.9bn (down low-single-digit q-o-q) in May, 
implying continued sequential weakness as the frenzy around Labubu fades, driving 
further earnings downgrades. Labubu (‘The Monsters’) contributed 38% of group revenue 
in 2025 (vs 40% in 2H25), underscoring its outsized role in the recent growth cycle. While 
broader IP expansion should partially offset this concentration, near-term growth is likely 
to fall below management targets and market expectations as the business rebases. We 
now forecast 2026 revenue growth of 14% y-o-y (vs 20% previously and below 
management’s >20% target) and cut 2026 non-IFRS net profit growth to 10% (from 18%). 
Our current 2026e net profit is 9% below consensus on a 7% below consensus revenue 
forecast, reflecting ongoing earnings risk. 
De-rating largely played out ahead of earnings cut: Compared with the previous 
earnings cycle, the valuation de-rating moved well ahead of fundamentals. In the last 
cycle (Mar-Nov 2022), the stock’s one-year forward PE compressed from 20.5x to 11x at 
the trough, before recovering to 15.7x at the end of the earnings downcycle. In the current 
cycle, despite earnings cuts only beginning in Mar 2026, the stock has already de-rated to 
11x forward PE, implying most multiple compression is priced in. From here, the share 
performance will hinge more on earnings stabilisation and re-acceleration, rather than a 
further de-rating. 
IP platform value to emerge post-cycle: While Labubu drove outsized growth over the 
past two years, the key question is whether Pop Mart can transition to platform-led growth 
from explosive growth of single-IP. Near term, the rebase of Labubu will weigh on 
momentum, but medium-term growth should be supported by broader IP monetarisation, 
new categories, and global retail rollout. As a category-defining IP platform, Pop Mart 
retains the ability to generate new hit IPs, supporting growth beyond 2026 and a potential 
re-rating once earnings bottom out.  
Maintain Buy; cut TP by 28.5% to HKD235.50 (from HKD329.50): Our lower target 
price is mainly driven by 7-9% cuts to our earnings estimates and using a higher WACC 
of 13.5% (before: 11.4%) to reflect near-term earnings uncertainty. Our long-term growth 
assumption of 3.0% remains unchanged. Our TP implies 2026e/27e P/E of 19.3x/15.4x 
and c51% upside to the current share price; we therefore retain our Buy rating.
