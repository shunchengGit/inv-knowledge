Hengrui announced a global strategic collaboration and licensing agreement with 
Bristol-Myers Squibb (BMS) yesterday (May 12), covering 13 early-stage projects 
spanning oncology, hematology, and immunology, with a potential total 
transaction value of up to US$15.2bn. This represents Hengrui’s largest BD 
transaction to date and is the second-largest China pharma out-licensing deal by 
total deal size — behind only AstraZeneca’s US$18.5bn licensing agreement with 
CSPC signed earlier in 2026. The deal also marks a meaningful strategic step-up 
from Hengrui’s prior landmark deal with GSK in 2025 (total deal size up to ~US
$12bn), which management described on yesterday’s investor call as the deal that 
validated the Chinese pharma-MNC alliance model; the BMS transaction builds 
on that foundation by introducing Co-Co optionality and joint early-stage 
discovery that the GSK deal did not include. We see the capital markets reacted 
decisively: H-shares surged as much as 15% intraday before settling at 
approximately +4.8% on the announcement (vs. HSHCI: -0.5%), while A-shares 
also gained 5% (vs. CSI Healthcare -0.5%). The deal is subject to US HSR antitrust 
clearance, with closing expected in 3Q26. We believe this deal not only showcases 
Hengrui’s comprehensive and deep early pipeline, but also once again confirms 
that China's innovative drug industry has differentiated drug discovery and early-
stage clinical development capabilities, valued by MNC pharma companies. The 
deal suggests BMS is not concerned about the news that the US House has asked 
the FDA to not consider clinical data from China for IND (investigational new 
drug) applications (see our note). We expect more similar deals could occur 
between China biotech/pharma and MNCs in the future. We reiterate our OW/N 
ratings for Hengrui’s A/H shares.
•
The collaboration is organized into three modules, with the earliest P&L 
contribution expected in 4Q26. Module 1 consists of four Hengrui-originated 
oncology and hematology assets with FIC (first-in-class) or BIC (best-in-
class) potential, for which BMS acquires exclusive ex-Greater China 
development, manufacturing, and commercialization rights. Module 2 
consists of four BMS-originated immunology assets, also carrying FIC/BIC 
profiles, for which Hengrui receives exclusive Greater China rights. Module 
3 consists of five wholly new co-developed projects to be built from scratch on 
Hengrui’s proprietary technology platforms, with both parties jointly selecting 
targets, designing R&D plans, and sharing development responsibilities from 
the discovery stage onward. Hengrui additionally retains Co-Co optionality: 
the right to elect into co-development on selected assets and participate in co-
commercialization within BMS territories, providing potential upside 
economics beyond milestone payments and royalties (although mgmt. has not 
disclosed which assets might be developed under the Co-Co structure).

•
The deal is structured to extract the complementary strengths of both parties and 
could drive further globalization for Hengrui as its first co-co deal. For Hengrui, the 
transaction monetizes its pre-clinical pipeline at a significant valuation, provides direct 
access to BMS’s three proprietary discovery platforms — targeted protein degradation, 
cell therapy, and radioligand therapy — through the co-development module, and it 
brings four BMS immunology assets into Hengrui’s China portfolio, selected specifically 
for their mechanistic complementarity with Hengrui’s existing immunology pipeline. 
The Co-Co structure further gives Hengrui access to BMS’s global clinical and 
commercial infrastructure, enabling the company to build global development 
capabilities. Management described this arrangement as providing hands-on exposure to 
leading international standards in clinical development, regulatory strategy, and 
commercial execution. For BMS, the collaboration provides efficient access to one of 
China’s most productive drug-development engines: Hengrui fields over 5,600 R&D 
personnel across 15 global research centers, maintains over 100 clinical-stage products, 
operates more than 10 proprietary technology platforms including ADC, bispecific 
antibody, and protein degradation, and produces approximately 20 FIC/BIC new 
molecules entering clinical development annually. This pipeline depth and execution 
speed is directly responsive to BMS’s strategic need to replenish its early-stage portfolio 
ahead of looming patent expiries. The clinical execution model further reinforces the fit: 
for all eight licensed assets across Modules 1 and 2, Hengrui will lead early clinical 
development through proof-of-concept, leveraging China’s large patient population and 
its clinical operations strength to accelerate PoC timelines. Management confirmed that 
these China-run PoC (proof of concept) studies are specifically designed to generate data 
capable of supporting global registrational trials — meaning they are calibrated to feed 
BMS’s ex-China Ph3 programs directly. BMS in turn provides its overseas clinical 
network to support ex-China early development.
•
Beyond the BMS partnership, the near-term pipeline catalyst stack is also broad. 
For ASCO’26, Hengrui has secured multiple oral presentations, with the key watch items 
being updated data on SHR-A2102 (Nectin-4) ADC, SHR-A1811 (HER2 ADC), 
camrelizumab in GI oncology, and its newer CLDN18.2/4-1BB bispecific antibody 
SHR-3821.We are also expecting key Ph3 readouts in the next 12 months, including: 
SHR-A1811 (HER2 ADC) in ovarian cancer and BTC (biliary tract cancer), SHR-A2009 
(HER3 ADC) in 2L EGFRm NSCLC, HER2-low breast cancer, and 1L HER2+ BC, 
HRS-7535 (oral GLP-1) in T2D (type 2 diabetes).

Investment Thesis, Valuation and Risks
Hengrui - A (Overweight; Price Target: Rmb70.00)
Investment Thesis
Hengrui-A is one of the largest pharma companies in China in terms of market cap. We like 
Hengrui’s strong R&D portfolio in China, solid sales capabilities, and sustained global 
business development potential. Also, we believe Hengrui could still enjoy a beta run if the 
overall market can maintain momentum from potential fiscal stimulus. After the recent 
correction, we believe Hengrui-A’s valuation is more digestible and see it as a good time to 
buy the stock. We rate Hengrui-A OW. 
Valuation
Our Dec-26 PT of Rmb70 is based on a DCF valuation. We estimate free cash flow for 
Hengrui until 2033, assuming a terminal growth rate of 3% and a WACC of 9.6%.
DCF valuation
Source: J.P. Morgan estimates.
Risks to Rating and Price Target
Key downside risks to our rating and price target include: 1) PD-1 marketing application 
being rejected by the FDA; and 2) clinical data of its ADC program coming in below our 
expectations. Key upside risks include stronger-than-expected sales growth and earnings.
