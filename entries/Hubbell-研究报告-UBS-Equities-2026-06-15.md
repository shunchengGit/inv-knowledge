Getting a bit more charged up?
Summary
The key debate around HUBB is whether its recent organic growth acceleration can be 
sustained, or will it moderate and indicate HUBB has good electrification exposures, but 
without the financial profile to match. In this report we provide our expectations around 
2Q results, and also lay out HUBB's prospective growth potential. We think that HUBB's 
end markets could translate to high single digit organic sales growth over time, but for 
now we'd like to see more evidence of topline momentum at the company. Specifically, 
we look for indications around the sustainability of improved electric distribution 
investment and more growth tied to volumes rather than price. HUBB is not the only 
company raising price, but we think that volume will need to be a larger contributor to 
growth before sentiment can turn more positive on the stock. Remain Neutral.
Can HUBB sustain high single digit organic sales growth?
We are Neutral-rated on HUBB with a PT of $515. The number of conversations we are 
having about HUBB is increasing. After a period of lagging other industrial equities with 
electrification exposure, we think that the next couple quarters will indicate whether 
HUBB can sustain higher growth into 2027 and if it can, it could be an  undervalued 
opportunity. For example, we estimate a $660 share price in an upside scenario 
(excluding NSI) ) vs. our base case price target of $515. 
Based on our conversations we think those more constructive on HUBB believe 8-9% 
organic growth is sustainable. We think this kind of growth, if HUBB were to achieve it,  
would be a meaningful catalyst for the equity value, as it would drive positive earnings 
revisions (cons models 5-6% in 2027-2028) and potentially a re-rating in valuation (at 
24x NTM P/E HUBB shares trade below broader industrials at 25x). Based on end market 
assumptions, we could see a path to 7-8% growth going forward, led by continued 
electric utility spending growth, data center strength, and GDP-type growth in non-res 
and industrial end markets (see Figure 1). Note that our actual forecast of 6% organic 
sales growth in 2027 is roughly in-line with consensus, and  is below this level as we 
would like to see more execution to get more confidence that higher growth rates can 
continue, hence we remain Neutral.
In a potential scenario in which HUBB delivers 8-9% organic sales growth annually, as 
some investors expect,  with an incremental margin of 35%, we project EPS could be 
$24 (2Q27-1Q28). Note that this excludes NSI. We think that this kind of operating 
performance would justify a re-rating in shares from 23x to 27x, resulting in a stock price 
forecast of $660.
