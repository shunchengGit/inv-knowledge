Alphabet reported strong results, with growth across all major segments, 
highlighted by significant outperformance in Cloud and strong results in Search. 
Consolidated revenues grew to $109.9bn, +22% y/y (+19% FXN), outperforming 
the street by a significant margin. Capex guidance for FY26 was increased by 
another $5bn at the mid-point, and the company expects FY27 capex to increase 
significantly compared to 2026, we think the resulting infrastructure footprint 
creates a meaningful moat that few (if any) can replicate. We believe Alphabet can 
best monetize this via the combination of its broad service offering (both 
advertising and subscriptions) as well as through the rapidly accelerating Cloud 
business.
On Search, AI features like AI Overviews and AI Mode are driving overall Search 
growth, with queries at an all-time high. These new features drive improvements 
in ads quality, a better understanding of user intent, new AI user experiences, and 
AI-powered advertiser tools. This is already contributing to accelerated Search & 
other revenue growth, which was up 19% to $60.4bn, driven primarily by the retail 
and finance verticals. On the monetization side, more than 30% of customer search 
spend now uses AI-enabled campaigns like AI Max or Performance Max. This is an 
important growth vector, as AI (specifically Gemini) significantly expands the ability 
to deliver ads on longer, more complex searches that were previously difficult to 
monetize. As such, even modest improvements in identifying and capitalizing on 
commercial-adjacent queries could unlock meaningful incremental growth 
opportunities for Google.
Cloud was a standout this quarter. While the y/y growth acceleration to 63% was 
encouraging, we were even more impressed by the near-doubling of the backlog 
sequentially to over $460bn, highlighting the outsized demand for GCP services. 
This backlog increase was driven by strong demand for enterprise AI offerings and 
the inclusion of TPU hardware sales. The company expects to recognize a small 
percentage of this TPU hardware revenue later in FY26, with the majority to be 
realized in FY27. Additionally, the acquisition of Wiz, a leading cloud and security 
AI platform, closed in March and will be reported within the Google Cloud segment.
