Soft 1Q26 dragged by NEV but 
Automation solid; 2Q26E could improve
Inovance reported 2025 and 1Q26 results in the evening of 27 April 2026 and hosted 
an analyst presentation on 28 April. The company has adjusted its segment disclosure 
and we have made corresponding adjustments in our model.
The -23% YoY net profit decline in 1Q26 is disappointing, dragged down 
predominantly by the NEV business. That said, this well-anticipated negative 
event has now passed. We reiterate our BUY recommendation and highlight 
positive drivers, including: (1) potential acceleration of Automation revenue in 
2Q26E thanks to robust market momentum and expanded capacity — 1Q26 
revenue was constrained by production, and April orders remained strong; (2) 
Inovance has increased prices for Automation products and is working to pass 
on cost inflation for its NEV business, which bodes well for profitability in the 
upcoming quarters; (3) Inovance has received nominations to supply 
components to Chinese humanoid robot makers and plans to launch wheeled 
humanoid robots in August; and (4) new businesses such as digital energy, AI 
data center products, and AI solutions are in early stages but offer long-term 
growth potential. We have cut our estimates. Our target price is RMB84.
Disappointing 1Q26 dragged down by the NEV business; Core profit better than 
it appeared
In 2025, Inovance delivered revenue of RMB45.1bn (+22% YoY) and net profit of 
RMB5.05bn (+18% YoY; 11.2% NPM). The results are in line with the ranges of the 
profit alert provided in February. Both revenue and net profit reached a record high 
level, although profitability was still well below historic peak levels. By segment, 
NEV sales grew +26% YoY, Automation +23% YoY and Elevator +6% YoY.
In 1Q26, Inovance printed revenue of RMB10.1bn (+13% YoY) and net profit of 
RMB1bn (-23% YoY; 10% NPM). By segment, NEV sales grew +12% YoY, 
Automation +14% YoY and Elevator +8% YoY.
We consider 1Q26 net profit disappointing. However, we calculate the core profit 
(i.e. excluding NEV business) has demonstrated resilience and was better than it 
appeared — refer to Figure 1 for detailed 2025 and 1Q26 results.

Outlook: Target 10-30% YoY revenue and net profit growth for 2026E, despite 
the profit decline in 1Q26; GPM could improve
For 2026E, Inovance targets 10-30% YoY growth for revenue and net profit. 
Management reassured that the targets have taken into consideration the 
challenges of the NEV business. Given the profit decline in 1Q26, this suggests 
strong profit growth in the reminder 9M of the year.
For 2Q26E, management guides for GPM to improve as Inovance raises prices for 
automation products. The NEV business is also trying to pass on cost inflation, 
although this comes with a lag. For revenue, management guides for higher 
revenue for the Automation business in 2Q26E on expanded production capacity.
We forecast group revenue of RMB53.8bn (+19% YoY), and net profit of RMB5.7bn 
(+13% YoY; 10.6% NPM) for 2026E.
Automation: Solid order momentum continued in April; 2Q26E to pick up as 
1Q26 affected by production constraints
Automation orders grew strongly by +40% YoY in 1Q26 for Inovance driven by 
broad-based end-market growth, led by battery, smart phone, machine tool, HVAC, 
automotive, construction machinery and electronics. Management pointed to 
continued solid momentum in April and believes market demand should be 
stronger in 2026E than in 2025. Out of the 40% order growth, management 
estimates 10% was driven by stock-up by distributors, with the majority of the 
growth still driven by underlying solid market demand.
Automation revenue growth of +14% YoY in 1Q26 was affected by production 
constraints. Inovance is increasing capacity and targets +30% higher production 
in May and June. Management therefore expects robust revenue delivery in 
2Q26E. Refer to Figure 5 for Inovance's automation orders vs revenue growth 
history.

Inovance has issued price increase letters twice YTD, one in January and one in 
March. Management believes Inovance has decent pricing power for a majority of 
customers. The higher memory price affects mainly PLCs. Management hears that 
some PLC models from certain international brands have run out of stock.
Comments about the solid China automation market momentum and price 
increases are consistent with our channel checks (link to our China Automation

We forecast +25% revenue growth YoY in the Automation business and GPM of 
44%, up ~50bps YoY in 2026E. We forecast revenue growth to accelerate to 30%

Humanoid robot: Nomination received from domestic OEMs to supply 
components; To launch wheeled robots in August
Inovance's product portfolio for humanoid robots include frameless motors, drives, 
encoders, screws, linear and rotary actuators and seven degrees of freedom robot 
arms.
For the components, Inovance has received nominations from local humanoid 
robot makers and are developing customised products for them. For robot arms, 
Inovance is able to mass produce these and is confident about the quality as the 
arms are equipped with liquid cooling and suitable for industrial use.
Inovance plans to launch wheeled robots for industrial application in August to 
address simple tasks such as handling, gripping, unpacking, door closing, and 
plugging and unplugging. Inovance targets to deploy them in its own and 
customers' factories.
Inovance is working with partners and supplies components and data. Inovance 
utilises existing large models rather than developing them itself.
As China's largest automation component manufacturer and China's top-four 
industrial robot maker with a comprehensive product portfolio, we think Inovance 
is well-positioned to become a prominent supplier for humanoid and AI robots in 
China over time.
NEV: Profit hit by cost inflation; Orders still strong; 2Q26E guided to be better 
than 1Q26
The NEV business disappointed the market in 1Q26 — NPM margin dropped to 
1.1% in 1Q26, representing the lowest NPM since 2023. This was due to the higher 
cost of copper, aluminum and rare earths which account for a relatively high portion 
of the COGS. Cost increases have exceeded the locked-in prices and hedging 
agreements. Furthermore, competition, product mix and R&D on new products 
also weighed on profits.
Management flagged strong orders, despite the slow sales in 1Q26. In 1Q26, 
Inovance secured multiple new project nominations from domestic and 
international OEMs for motors, electronic controls, power supplies, and assembly 
products (overseas projects being global platform projects). Additionally, the 800V 
active suspension product for a domestic OEM will enter the SOP (start of 
production) stage soon.

AI: Integrating AI with Inovance's industrial know-how
Inovance completed the development of "Inovance Industrial Intelligent Brain 
(iFG)" platform in 1H25. iFG integrates data, models and agents. Inovance targets 
to implement AI from the below perspectives:
n
Applying iFG on existing software and hardware offerings (CNC, PLC, HMI, 
etc.) to conduct intelligent Q&A, code generation and intelligent 
interaction;
n
Empowering discrete automation customers with agents;
n
Providing AI edge all-in-one machine for diagnosis, prediction 
maintenance, and efficiency improvement.
Management did not provide long-term targets but expects revenue from AI to 
double its current software revenue in 3-5 years.
We feel that it is still early days for Inovance to promote its AI solutions. In contrast, 
we think its peer Supcon started the AI initiatives much earlier and has a clearer 
path towards monetisation — Supcon targets RMB800mn-1bn revenue from 
Industrial AI in 2026E. We believe Inovance should benefit from the industrial data 
it possesses and its industrial know-how to link AI with the physical world. We 
therefore will monitor its progress closely.
Digital energy: A top five supplier for medium- to high-power PCS globally
Inovance supplies power conversion system (PCS). In 2025, Inovance generated 
RMB500mn revenue from the PCS business. Frost & Sullivan reckons Inovance is 
the fifth largest supplier for medium- to high-power PCS globally by shipment 
volume in 2025 with a market share of 7%. The company announced on 17 
November that it has commissioned a new plant for PCS in Xi'an. The designed 
capacity is 50GW and could contribute RMB10bn to revenue. Additionally, 
Inovance has the capability for energy management for commercial and industrial 
customers.
For energy storage, Inovance shipped 10GW and over 1GWh storage in 2025. It has 
established model points in 20+ countries in 2025 and plans to further expand the 
business.
AI Data Centre: Supplying PSU, and developing SST
Inovance already supplies power supply units (PSU) for data centres. It is also 
forming up a team to develop solid-state transformer (SST). Management also sees 
opportunities in energy management considering its leading place in inverters.

Cut estimates; Target price at RMB84
We cut our 2026/27 net profit forecasts by -8%/ -4%, respectively. We introduce 
new 2028 forecasts.
We slightly adjust our DCF-derived target price to RMB84 from RMB83.9 as the 
lower estimates are offset by us rolling forward the valuation by 3M. Our target price 
of RMB84 is made up of (1) RMB80.2 (from RMB80) per share, based on a three-
stage DCF of the underlying business, and (2) RMB3.8 (from RMB3.9) per share 
from humanoid robot contribution totalling ~RMB10bn. We use a terminal growth 
rate of 3.5% given the structural growth in the automation and NEV market. We use 
a risk-free rate of 1.8%, an equity risk premium of 5%, and a beta of 1.2 to derive our 
WACC of 7.7%.
Inovance is trading on 2026E P/E of 32.1x and 2027E P/E of 25.9x, below its average 
long-term one-year-forward P/E of 34.3x.
Downside risks include: 1) slow automation market growth; 2) lack of share gain of 
Inovance for the automation business; 3) severe pricing pressure weighing on 
margins; and (4) a slow NEV market and the resultant pressure on its automotive 
sales and profitability.
