• We summarize key takeaways after China telecom operators’ 1Q26 results. 
Overall, CM/CT/CU’s service revenue was -1%/-2%/-1% YoY in 1Q26, with 
net profit -4%/-17%/-18%, respectively, due mainly to the impact from the 
VAT adjustment. CM’s profit decline is in line with our estimate, while CT/CU 
are below our estimates (JPMe -7% YoY each for 2026E profit), due to higher 
personnel costs. Cash flow is a bright spot: CM’s 1Q26 FCF of Rmb41bn is 
substantially stronger than the Rmb5bn outflow in 1Q25, while CT’s 1Q26 FCF 
of Rmb8.4bn is much stronger than Rmb3.4bn outflow in 1Q25. This 
demonstrates China telecom operators' focus on growth quality and should 
support sustainable dividend payouts, in our view. On the industrial internet 
side, China Telecom Cloud revenue was +7% YoY, and China Unicom 
compute power revenue was +8% YoY in 1Q26. 
• Our order of preference among H-share names is CM-H > CT-H > CU-H: 
We note that CM-H/CT-H’s share prices have been resilient post the 1Q26 
results. We believe the key rationales are: CM-H (solid large-cap high yielder at 
6% dividend yield on 2026E), CT-H (potentially due to strong sentiment on 
cloud/data center sector post DeepSeek V4 launch recently, which confirms the 
practical feasibility of domestic chips for frontier AI model inference. Please 
refer to the report on China AI from Olivia Xu. Our order of preference is now 
based mainly on affordability of dividend payout and cloud exposure. We prefer 
CM-H the most, as it has the mildest impact from VAT adjustment in 2026 (due 
to its substantially higher margin than peers), and its dividend yield of 6.2% in 
2026E (on JPMe) is largest among the Big 3. While CT-H is the most expensive 
of the three (trades at 16x 2026E P/E if we assume similar profit YoY decline 
for full year 2026E as 1Q26), its positioning as a ‘cloud play’ among the big 
three telcos (25% of total service revenue in 2025, vs 12%/21% of CM-H/CU-
H) makes it a potential beneficiary of the China domestic cloud thematic trade. 
We prefer CU-H least as it trades at a similar P/E as CM-H (12x 2026E if we 
assume similar profit YoY decline for full year 2026 as 1Q26) and the 
likelihood for higher dividend payout ratio in 2026E is low (due to profit impact 
from VAT).
