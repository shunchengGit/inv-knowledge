Memory visibility extends to 2027 while Foundry sees an earlier 
breakeven 
 
30 APRIL 2026 
Securities Research Report 
Production time: 19:14* (London time) 
Research Analysts & Publishing Entities 
1Q26 booked a record high quarterly revenue of KRW134trn (+43% q-q) and operating profit of 
KRW57trn (+185% q-q) thanks to memory super-cycle. ASP of DRAM/NAND surged by low-90%/ 
high-80% q-q while bit shipment was up single digit q-q due to supply constraints. We lift EPS by
81%/ 33%/ 15% for 2026E/ 27E/ 28E mainly on high memory prices. We raise TP to KRW300,000, 
using SOTP valuation (unchanged method), which implies 2027E P/E / P/B of 8.5x/2.1x. 
Non-HBM margin surprised in 1Q; new products rolling out 
Conventional DRAM margins exceeded HBM in 1Q26 as quarterly contracts captured price hike
faster than annual contracts for HBM in supply constraints. Samsung shall benefit more with high 
conventional DRAM exposure before the gap narrows in 2027. It continues to lead memory tech, 
with sample shipment or qualification process start for HBM4E, SOCAMM2, PCle Gen6 SSD etc.
from 2Q. 
Foundry strengthening by advanced nodes and overseas fab ramping-up 
Samsung’s Foundry revenue posted >10% y-y growth in 1Q and secured a key silicon photonics 
project. We expect the Foundry business to break even in early 2027, driven by (1. high utilisation of 
4nm node from high demand for HBM4 base dies, and ramp-up of memory products and LPU; and 
(2. mass production of Gen2 2nm mobile product in 2H26. Longer term, we expect 2nm mass
production at Taylor Fab to effectively expand the US client base and boost margin from 2028E. 
Reclaim no.1 in global smartphone market while margin to deteriorate 
Despite global smartphone market contraction from seasonality and cost inflation, MX division grew 
q-q in rev and operating profit, thanks to the higher ASP from newly launched flagship S26 series 
and high-end “Ultra” model. A secured memory supply supported Samsung to reclaim global no.1 in 
smartphone shipment, gaining share from Chinese players. But we remain cautious that smartphone 
margin will decline sequentially from 2Q until the memory cycle moderates from early 2027E.

Key takeaways from SK Hynix 1Q26 results 
briefing 
1Q26 snapshot: 
Memory: DRAM bit growth met guidance of low-single digit q-q. NAND exceeded bit 
shipment guidance (mid-single digit q-q) with growth of high-single digit q-q. Server 
DRAM bit shipment grew in low-teens q-q, and NAND bit shipments grew in the low-20% 
range q-q. Blended ASP for DRAM/NAND rose by low-90%/high-80% q-q. 
System LSI: 1Q earnings improved q-q thanks to expanded sales of flagship SoC. 
MX: MX secured single-digit profitability growth q-q by offsetting rising memory 
component costs with proactive resource efficiency. 
SDC: 1Q performance declined q-q due to seasonality and the downstream impact of 
high memory price pressure on smartphone OEMs. Large displays remained stable, 
supported by gaming monitor demand. 
VD & DA: 1Q TV demand declined post-holidays, though premium segments (Neo QLED, 
OLED) held firm. Operating profit improved q-q but fell y-y due to stagnant demand and 
rising raw material costs.  
Outlook 
Memory: 2Q bit growth will be single-digit q-q for DRAM and constrained to low-single-
digits for NAND due to tight 1Q inventory. 
Foundry: the Taylor Fab has completed equipment move-in, targeting operations in 2026 
and mass production in 2027. Uncompetitive mature 8-inch lines are being aggressively 
phased out, transitioning capacity to 17nm nodes for high-barrier specialty demand. 
System LSI: 2Q revenue is expected to dip on seasonal headwinds. In 2H26, the division 
aims to secure new design wins for Exynos 2700, reinforce its 200 megapixel image 
sensor lineup, and strategically pivot toward specialized inference solutions and custom 
SoC for AI data centers and on-device applications.  
MX: 2Q revenue and profitability will decline q-q as seasonality sets in and component 
cost pressures intensify. For 2H26, while total shipment is expected to drop due to rising 
costs, MX projects revenue growth by pushing its super-premium products, expanding 
Galaxy AI features, and exploring new multi-modal form factors (e.g., AI glasses). 
SDC: 2Q IT demand will remain weak. In 2H26, Samsung will defend profitability through 
premium smartphone technology and accelerate revenue growth through the ramp-up of 
Gen 8.6 IT OLED line. 
VD &DA: 2Q demand will be buoyed by major international sporting events via products 
such as new Micro LED and Mini LED launches. In 2H26, VD aims to pioneer the AI TV 
market and expand high-margin advertising/ licensing services. DA is restructuring for 
profitability and officially entering the high-growth data center cooling market following its 
acquisition of FlaktGroup, with plans for North American and Korean expansion.  
Capex: KRW11.2trn was spent in 1Q, with KRW10.2 to DS and KRW0.6trn to display 
division. Samsung anticipates a substantial y-y increase in 2026 Capex to deploy new 
cleanroom capacity and support Taylor Fab ramp-ups. Over KRW110trn is planned for 
future facilities and R&D.

We raise TP to KRW300,000 
We raised Samsung EPS forecasts by 81%/ 33%/ 15% for 2026E/27E/28E primarily on 
higher memory price. Given the continuous supply constraints on both high price HBM 
and conventional memory, Samsung shall benefit more with high exposure to 
conventional DRAM before the gap narrows in 2027 as it remains the largest supplier of 
both DRAM and NAND. We lift ASP forecast of DRAM and NAND for Samsung by 66%/ 
30%/ 19% and 59%/ 20%/ flat in 2026E/27E/28E.  Margin of DRAM and NAND thus go 
up by15pp/ 8pp/ 6pp and 16pp/ 5pp/ flat accordingly, and memory contributes to nearly 
90% operating profit, in line with historical trend of memory upcycles.  
We arrive at our new TP by using SOTP, an EV/EBITDA valuation method for each 
business division and applying a 10% conglomerate discount (unchanged). We use 17x/ 
3.6x/ 17.6x/ 4.9x 2027 EV/EBITDA multiples for Samsung’s DX & Harman/ memory/ non-
memory DS/ SDC division (vs prev 15.8x/ 5.2x/ 13.1x/ 5.0x respectively). Our new TP of 
KRW300,000 implies 2027 P/E at 8.6x (vs prev 10.6x) and P/B at 2.1x (vs prev. 2.3x).
