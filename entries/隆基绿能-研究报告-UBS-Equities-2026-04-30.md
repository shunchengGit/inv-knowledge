Longi Green Energy Technology
Weak FY25/1Q26 results, but module is 
expected to turn profitable in 3Q26
Weak results in FY25/1Q26
Longi recorded net loss at Rmb6.4bn, in line with preliminary results. Excluding 
impairment loss,  1Q26 net loss slightly narrowed QoQ to Rmb1.7bn. 1Q26 blended 
GPM was down 1ppt QoQ to -1.2%, weighed by rising  silver cost and low utilization 
under weak demand and capacity upgrade. Longi delivered 111.6GW/20.5GW wafer 
shipment in FY25/1Q26, with external sales of 48.6GW/7.6GW. Module shipment was 
86.6GW/12.6GW  in FY25/1Q26. 
Positive outlook on module with technology upgrade
 Longi has 57GW BC capacity by end-25 (incl  46GW self-owned and  11GW from 
external partner). BC shipment reached 22.87/8.34GW in 2025/1Q26, with BC 
shipment mix up from 28% in 2025 to 66% in 1Q26. Longi expect BC could enjoy a US
$0.01-0.02/W price premium and a 10% GPM premium over Topcon at comparable 
cost. After capacity upgrade in March, Longi's BC  achieved cost parity with Topcon. 
Longi plan to upgrade 20GW capacity with silver consumption reduction and aim for 
8GW shipment in 2H26.  With further ramp-up of BC shipment and  continued cost 
reduction, mgmt expect module business  to turn profitable in 3Q26. 
Expansion in BESS business 
After the  equity acquisition of Jingkong Energy in January,  Longi is actively enhancing its 
product, sales network and service quality for the BESS business. Mgmt believe the 
company could leverage on its sales channel of solar business and industry insight on 
renewables to strengthen its edge in the BESS sector.  Longi target 6GWh BESS shipment 
in 2026, with a focus on China, Europe, Australia and US markets. Mgmt are ambitious 
to grow BESS business to the similar scale as the solar business in the next 5 years.
Valuation: Cut EPS and PT; Maintain Buy
We cut 2026-28E  EPS forecasts by 237%/71%/5% mainly on lower wafer and module 
margin assumption. Our DCF-based PT is cut from Rmb25.3 to Rmb22.8 (WACC= 
5.8%). Excluding Rmb26.4bn net cash, Longi is trading at 1.9x P/B, 1SD below historical 
average. We maintain a Buy on Longi as we believe the ample cash, differentiated BC 
technology and synergy between solar and BESS business could make Longi more 
resilient in the industry downturns.

Company Description
Longi Green Energy Technology (Longi) is the largest monocrystalline wafer manufacturer in 
the world by capacity. The company has expanded from upstream monocrystalline wafer 
manufacturing to mid-stream monocrystalline solar cell and module production and 
downstream solar power station development. At end-2024, Longi achieved monocrystalline 
wafer/solar cell/module capacity of 185GW/105GW/135GW.
Valuation Method and Risk Statement
Our price target for Longi is based on DCF methodology. Risks to our valuation include: 1) 
slowing global solar demand; 2) slower-than-expected progress in new technology; 3) more 
intense price competition than we previously expected.
