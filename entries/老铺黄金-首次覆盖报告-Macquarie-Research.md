Macquarie Equity Research
11 May 2026
Laopu Gold
Initiation: As good as it can get
Key Points
• Among luxury brands in China, Laopu Gold has the highest revenue per
shopping mall and revenue per available square metre (in 2025).
• We have concerns that Chinese consumption power cannot match
frequent price hikes, resulting in volume growth moderating.
• We initiate coverage of Laopu at Underperform, with a HK$453 TP,
based on 10x 2027E PER.
Can demand outpace the gold price rally? Gold price rallied by >60% YoY
in 2025 and Laopu went through by four rounds of price hikes. We have
concerns that Chinese consumption power cannot match the frequent
price hikes over the last two years, resulting in volume growth moderation.
If the gold price remains rangebound, gold jewellery purchase demand
is likely to moderate. Meanwhile, frequent discounts in 2025 could be a
potential risk to Laopu's brand building.
Growth moderation from a high base and rising competition. Laopu
same-store sales reached Rmb600-700mn per year in 2025. We saw limited
upside since the store efficiency has exceeded top international luxury
brands. Laopu is a first-mover in promoting heritage gold consumption in
China; its success also attracts competitors to the space, which is likely be a
headwind diluting store efficiency. Its operating expense ratio was only 14%
in FY25, vs >40% for international peers. We think it is likely to see more
channel and brand building investment in the future. As the company’s
store size keeps expanding, we are concerned operating deleverage will be
severe once sales growth moderates.
Dilemma between growth and capital management. At the end of 2025,
the company had Rmb4.2bn net debt or Rmb6.26bn interest-bearing
borrowings. We believe it could stretch its balance sheet to further expand
its network. The company declared no less than a 50% dividend payout,
implying 6.7% cash dividend yield for FY26E.
Earnings: We expect 140% net profit growth in FY26E, while in FY27E/28E
we expect -40%/1%, due to SSS decline and operating deleveraging.
Valuation: We initiate coverage of Laopu at Underperform, with a HK$453
TP, based on industry average multiple of 10x 2027E PER.
Catalysts: Earnings release, overseas expansion, operational update.
Investment Thesis and Recommendation
We initiate coverage of Laopu at Underperform as we expect its superior
store efficiency may not be sustainable amid competition and frequent
price hikes, and potential operating deleverage risks.

Macquarie Equity Research
Laopu Gold
Company overview
Founded in 2009, Laopu Gold is the first gold jewellery brand in China to promote the
concept of heritage gold, and the first to apply heat treatment of enamels (燒藍) to
pure gold products. These designs carry deep symbolic meaning and offer high product
recognition. In addition to jewellery, the company specialises in high-weight gold items such
as collectable pieces, everyday-use goldware, and decorative objects.
As of December 2025, Laopu Gold operated a total of 45 stores, including 39 self-operated
stores across 13 major cities in mainland China, 3 stores in Hong Kong, 2 stores in Macau and
1 store in Singapore.
Frost & Sullivan estimates the market size of heritage gold jewellery in China surged from
c.Rmb13.0bn in 2018 to c.Rmb219bn in 2024, accounting for 24% of China's total jewellery
market share, and forecasts it to deliver an 18% revenue CAGR over 2024-2028E.
Moderating growth from high base
Laopu Gold has historically relied heavily on high same-store sales growth to drive its overall
revenue performance. This strategy was effective during the early stages of development,
particularly as the company benefited from its first-mover advantage in the heritage gold
segment, strong product innovation, and Chinese chic-brand positioning strategy.
According to the company, in 2025, Laopu achieved an average annualised sales amount (tax-
inclusive) of nearly Rmb1bn per shopping mall. According to Frost & Sullivan, among global
luxury groups in 2025, it ranked first in terms of revenue per shopping mall and revenue per
available square metre in China.
However, we believe the strong gold price rally is one of the most important drivers for Laopu
Gold's market-share gains in the jewellery market. While we appreciate the company's unique
product strategy, refined product craftsmanship and brand positioning, we expect limited
upside for its average single store sales due to 1) sales volume growth moderation amid
a surging gold price; and 2) Laopu's store efficiency has exceeded top international luxury
brands.
•
Based on historical Chow Tai Fook's gold products SSSG, we see a negative correlation
between gold sales volume and the gold-price trend. We are concerned that Chinese
consumers’ purchasing power has not kept pace with frequent price hikes, leading to further
sales volume deterioration. Even if gold prices stabilise or decline, this would still be a weak
consumption backdrop, as consumers primarily buy gold jewellery for value preservation.
Is operating leverage sustainable?
Laopu is benefiting from significant same-store sales growth, which creates substantial
operating leverage and fast margin expansion. However, despite accelerating top-line
performance, we see operating margin expansion and operating leverage benefits
moderating.
Meanwhile, average gross margin and operating margin of major luxury/jewellery brands
for FY25 is 65% and 24% respectively. Laopu is priced and performing like an optimised
"best-case scenario" luxury brand, with an operating margin higher than most luxury groups
except for Hermes, despite a much lower gross margin. As the company’s store size keeps
expanding, we believe any normalisation in China's "gold fever" or a moderation in its revenue
growth will lead to severe operating deleveraging risks.
Dilemma between growth and capital management. At the end of 2025, the company
had Rmb4.2bn net debt on its balance sheet and 45 stores worldwide. The company could
stretch its balance sheet further or fund raise to further expand its network.

Financials and valuations
Revenue
Our Macquarie Desk strategists forecast the average gold price to rise 58%/18% in 1H/2H26
YoY, and then decline 13%/7% YoY in 1H27/2H27, Thus, we expect fixed-price gold
momentum to slow in 2027.
•
For 2026E, we expect 35% SSSG, driven mainly by elevated gold prices and price hikes. We
forecast 83% revenue growth YoY, with 59% growth in offline boutiques and 200% growth
online, supported by strong SSSG, store expansion, and gold price momentum.
For 2027E, we expect a 40% SSS decline as lower gold prices weigh on sales volumes. We
forecast a 20% revenue decline, with a 32% drop in boutique sales, partly offset by 12%
online revenue growth.
