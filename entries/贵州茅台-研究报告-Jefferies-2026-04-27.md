Positioned for Structural Growth from 2H26
We reiterate our positive view on Moutai's shares after 2025 and 1Q26
results. We view the share price pullback as an opportunity to buy into
the structural growth that may drive earnings turnaround in 2H26. We also
highlight Moutai's near-term sentiment drivers including its good fit into the
"China luxury recovery" narrative as well as its defensiveness to raw material
price shocks. We update our model.
Part of China's recovering luxury market. Recent results by the luxury brands show stabilizing
demand and sentiment for luxury goods in China, in our view. We view Moutai as a good proxy
of China luxury consumption. In fact, our checks show a strong rebound in Moutai's retail sales
in the past 4-6 months with retail sales largely resuming positive YoY.
Immune to raw-material price shocks. Unlike other staple and alcohol companies, we believe
Moutai's high margins make it immune to raw-material cost shocks. In addition, utilities and
transportation costs appear insignificant to Moutai's margins. We do not see oil shortage as
a risk to Moutai's production.
Channel reform to drive 2H26 earnings rebound. We estimate Moutai to accelerate its market
share gain in major premium baijiu price ranges (above RMB1,000 per bottle, 600-1,000,
400-600), thanks to:
(1) On-line and off-line integration: Moutai's greater control of Feitian prices has prevented the
on-line off-line resell activities (traders buying Feitian on-line and sell them in the off-line). This
allows Moutai to better utilize the on-line channel: to enjoy the traffic as well as subsidies from
the e-commerce platforms. We believe Moutai is the first mover to embrace e-commerce and
quick commerce in the baijiu industry. This should help Moutai take market share from its
lagging competitors.
(2) Distributor incentives: Despite Moutai's DTC push, we expect distributors to remain
encouraged. The product overhaul of Moutai 1935 and Moutai Prince is proven to be
successful, in our view. This facilitates distributors' new sources of earnings, in additional to
traditionally only relying on the Feitian. As a result, distributors are now incentivized to promote
Moutai's full range of products, based on our checks. Moutai's positive distributor sentiment
and margins is a rare trait in today's baijiu industry, and should help Moutai take market share,
in our view.
Model updates. The demand shock from adverse policy in 2H25 has led to greater-than-
expected impacts on Moutai's 2H25 earnings. We cut our forecasts and DCF-based PT
accordingly. Having said that, Moutai's earnings continue to be much more resilient than the
>30% YoY decline seen at other baijiu peers. In the longer-term, we continue to believe that
Moutai will deliver steady sales growth and operating leverage while freeing up more cash.
This should enable a stronger free cash flow CAGR than top-line.

 The Long View: Kweichow Moutai
Investment Thesis
• Moutai is the dominant player in China's super-premium liquor market,
with its brand equity among the strongest.
• We estimate Moutai's latest channel strategy (to give the market back to
large distributors) will help stabilize the prices of Feitian.
• We forecast mid- to high-single-digit volume growth for the next three
years. Price hikes and product mix changes are likely to be the key drivers
of sales growth.
• In addition to the flagship Feitian Moutai product, we also expect
sustainable growth for peripheral Moutai products, including 1935.

Sustainability Matters
Top Material Issue(s): 1) Food safety and product quality are the most important for F&B
manufacturers. The company adheres to the 'Five Craftsmanship' philosophy and built its '365' quality
management system. 2) Environment protection is also a key issue for baijiu manufacturers as they
use water as a major resource and impact the ecosystem around their factories. Natural gas, water,
slag, green environment, carbon and other ecological factors all matter. 3) Corruption is an issue that
high-end baijiu companies need to face.
Company Target(s): 1) Starting from 2014, the company plans to invest RMB50 million per year for 10
consecutive years (RMB500 million in total) for ecosystem protection of the Chishui River basin. Buffer
zones and construction-prohibited zones for interim and long-term ecological environment protection
have been planned. 2) Comply with SDGs.
Qs to Mgmt: 1) How do you balance ecological conservation with the capacity expansion plan? 2)
What level of environmental protection costs do you expect to incur in the mid-long term?
