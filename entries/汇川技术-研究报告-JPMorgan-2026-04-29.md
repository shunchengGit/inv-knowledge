Style Exposure
Inovance’s 1Q26 earnings missed despite very strong IA order growth, and the past 
two sessions show the market’s focus has tightened to one issue, in our view: 
whether order momentum converts into delivered revenue and margin recovery 
fast enough to defend FY26 guidance. The stock fell ~6% yesterday on the print 
versus a flattish CSI300, then rebounded 10% today following the after-market 
briefing (CSI300 again flattish at +0.7%), as investors gained comfort from 
yesterday’s briefing that the shortfall was mainly timing/high base and one-offs 
rather than demand deterioration, and that delivery bottlenecks have a near-term 
operational path to improvement. We maintain OW but de-risk our forecasts: 
FY26/27E profit cut by 7%/10%, FY28E introduced, DCF horizon extended to 
Jun-27, and PT reset to Rmb84 (~25% upside post today’s rebound), supported by 
~15% revenue CAGR and ~23% profit CAGR into FY28E.
•
IA demand broad-based and April order momentum strong, yet 1Q 
delivery lagged materially on capacity constraint. The near-term setup is 
binary in terms of investor confidence, we believe: (i) capacity uplift in May 
(30%+) and a visible 2Q backlog digestion cycle that translates into revenue 
booking; and (ii) an initial turn in margin trajectory as price pass-through and 
product mix begin to show up more clearly. Pricing power remains an 
important differentiator, but it needs to reflect in reported gross margin; the 
management briefing points to March–April as the start of IA price-effect 
visibility, making 2Q the first quarter where consolidated GPM is poised to 
recover with IA leading. We believe the share price whipsaw (-6% then +10% 
vs flat CSI300) reinforces that the market will reward proof quickly if these 
checkpoints land.
•

Summary Investment Thesis and Valuation
Investment Thesis
We rate Shenzhen Inovance OW, given its superior supply chain 
management and product development capabilities, which 
position it to capture China’s ongoing factory automation and 
industrial upgrade. Inovance’s consistent track record of returns 
and robust Op-CF generation stand out among peers, providing 
comfort for investors amid rising macro headwinds. Beyond its 
core strengths in FA, Inovance is also actively investing in new 
growth drivers—including humanoid robots, energy storage 
systems (ESS), and digital/AI platforms—which are set to 
support the next phase of expansion and margin resilience. We 
believe Inovance is distinctively positioned to benefit from the 
ongoing upcycle in China’s FA sector, with its ability to deliver 
innovation, scale, and execution making it the best play for 
investors seeking exposure to this structural transformation.
Valuation
Our DCF-based Jun-27 PT of Rmb84 is based on terminal 
growth of 3% and a WACC of 8%, calculated based on the 
following assumptions: cost of equity at 9.0% (based on Rf = 
2.2% [aligned with the latest China 10-year bond yield], an 
equity risk premium of 6.8% and a beta of 1.0 x), cost of debt at 
~4% and target debt-to-capital of 20%. Our PT translates into a 
target PEG of c1.5x against our projected 23% earnings growth 
over FY25-28E.

Model update post-1Q26 briefing: Order momentum and FY26 
guidance remain intact
1Q26 earnings were a miss despite very strong order growth, and the share price 
reaction over the past two days usefully isolates what the market is (and is not) willing 
to pay for at this stage of the cycle: investors were initially positioned to penalize 
“delivery/margin conversion” risk, but they were also quick to re-rate once the after-
market briefing narrowed the uncertainty around the bridge from orders to recognized 
revenue and profit. The stock fell ~6% yesterday on the print versus a flattish CSI300, 
reflecting near-term disappointment and lingering skepticism after consecutive quarters 
where headline delivery has not kept pace with the strength implied by the upcycle. 
However, after the after-market briefing, the stock rebounded ~10% today (CSI300 
again flattish), as investors appeared more comfortable that the miss was not demand-
led but instead driven by timing (order-to-revenue recognition lag), a high base and one-
off items, and that the current delivery pressure is operationally addressable via near-
term capacity actions—i.e., the problem set shifted from “is the cycle real?” to “how 
quickly can Inovance convert backlog into reported numbers and stabilize margins?” In 
that context, the reiterated FY26 guidance of 10–30% Y/Y growth for both revenue and 
profit reads as more credible than it did immediately post-results, even if the implied 
path still requires a meaningful step-up in performance through the remaining quarters, 
and therefore leaves little room for additional execution slippage.
We remain OW, but we are explicitly de-risking the forecast profile to reflect: (i) a more 
conservative margin recovery cadence versus the optimistic “snapback” path the market 
would likely need to see to fully rebuild confidence; and (ii) the reality that even with 
strong orders, the translation to P&L can remain noisy when capacity, product mix and 
cost pass-through are moving targets. Specifically, we cut FY26/27E profit by 7%/10%, 
and introduce FY28E to better capture the medium-term compounding from automation 
penetration, overseas expansion and new engines (software/AI, robotics, ESS energy 
storage, and PA process automation adjacencies). 
We extend our valuation horizon to Jun-27. Our DCF-based PT resets to Rmb84 (from 
Rmb97), which implies ~25% upside from current levels even after today’s rebound, 
underpinned by our projected ~15% revenue CAGR and ~23% profit CAGR into 
FY28E. We preserve our core medium-term investment case (structural share gains + 
platformization + multi-engine growth), while acknowledging that near-term sentiment 
will likely remain highly sensitive to proof points on delivery catch-up, margin 
stabilization and the “quality” of order growth—exactly the factors that drove the sharp 
de-rating yesterday and the equally sharp re-rating today.
