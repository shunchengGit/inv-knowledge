Horizon Robotics  | Asia Pacific
A Starry Debut - Integrated 
Smart Cockpit-driving Solution 
Arrives 
We attended Horizon’s Tech Day in Beijing today, where the 
company launched its integrated smart cockpit-driving solution 
Starry 6P along with HSD v1.6 and Kaka Claw, targeting 3Q26 
SOP. 
Long-awaited integrated smart cockpit-driving solution launched today: It is to 
run on their newly launched Starry 6P chip, which uses 5nm processing (first among 
domestic smart cockpit solution) and comes with 650 TOPS; the company also 
unveiled a more economical version Starry 6H with 500 TOPS. The integrated 
solution completed OEM validation in 1Q26 and is on track for mass production in 
3Q26, which would debut on Chery's iCar initially.
Less is more: Per the company, the integrated solution aims to deliver 50%+ 
reductions in space and units of components, and 56% shorter delivery time, 
compared to traditional smart cockpit + smart driving solutions. Meanwhile, it 
should be profit accretive to OEM customers by achieving Rmb1.5-4k technical cost 
savings from reductions in wires and shells, as well as Rmb2-3k cost savings in 
memory chips. The company mentioned that the Starry integrated solution is 
Rmb5k cheaper than a traditional solution that runs on Nvidia Thor AD chip + 
Qualcomm 8295 smart cockpit chip. 
One for all, all for one: Horizon's founder noted that BYD, Chery, Chang’an, BAIC, 
FAW, Volkswagen, Bosch, and Denso have all expressed strong interest in their 
Starry series.
A timely launch: As highlighted in our note published this March, although 
integrated smart cockpit-driving solution isn't a new concept, we noticed an 
increasing number of OEMs pivoting to integration this year, including EV startups 
that are developing it in-house. On one hand, we see growing synergy/interaction 
between smart cockpit systems and autonomous driving as we approach L3/L4, as 
both systems are requiring much higher AI computing needs; on the other hand, 
rising memory costs are incentivising OEMs to adopt integrated solutions.
Stay OW: We view Horizon Robotics as well-positioned to benefit from this trend, 
supported by 1) its solid ecosystem with leading OEMs BYD, Geely, and Chery; 2) 
well-rounded technology know-how in both hardware (J6 platform) and software 
(HSD), and 3) its early mover advantage among peers. For 2H26, we believe its 
integrated cockpit/driving solution rollout and robust J6P+HSD order pipeline could 
be key to fueling more meaningful share-price recovery.

Valuation Methodology and Risks
 Horizon Robotics (9660.HK)  
Probability-weighted DCF, with probability weightings of 25% bull case, 50% base case, 25% 
bear case. The balanced probabilities in our bull case vs. bear case reflect our constructive 
view on Horizon gaining volume market share among ADAS+AD players in China, but we are 
also aware of rising competition and potential geopolitical headwinds in the smart driving 
business. Key DCF assumptions: 12.2% WACC (1.9 beta, 3% long-term growth rate).
 Risks to Upside  
n Faster-than-expected ADAS/AD adoption growth in China
n Delays/failure in OEMs' in-house hardware design
n Narrowing access to other solutions providers in China
n Expanding customer base with additional key customers
 Risks to Downside  
n Slower-than-expected ADAS/AD adoption in China
n Supply chain disruption
n OEMs' successful in-house hardware design initiatives
n Vehicle sales pressure from key customers in China
