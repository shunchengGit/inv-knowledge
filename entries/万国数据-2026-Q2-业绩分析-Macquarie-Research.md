GDS Holdings
AI demand ignites solid beat
Key Points
• GDS' strong 1Q26 beat was driven by accelerated hyperscale cloud
onboarding and surging demand for high-performance AIDC resources.
• Net new bookings of ~200MW hit a quarterly record high, and is well on
track to surpass company's annual target of 500MW new bookings.
• Reiterate Outperform/Marquee Buy as a strong backlog enhances
revenue visibility into 2027E and onwards.
•
Strong 1Q26 results. Net revenue of Rmb3.4bn (+24% YoY) beat MQe
by 17% and Visible Alpha (VA) consensus by 13%. Adjusted EBITDA of
Rmb1.9bn (+47% YoY) beat MQe by 35% and VA by 37%.
•
Solid booking momentum in 1Q26. GDS secured 200MW of new
capacity as of 1Q26, placing it well on track to surpass its full-year
500MW target. The company continued to demonstrate superior
execution by securing multiple high-profile, large-scale commitments
from core customers, with management indicating a 500–800MW/year
pipeline over the next three years, with upside potential. In our view, GDS
is well positioned to capture this demand given its substantial land bank
of ~4GW, providing ample capacity for future development and sales
conversion. To support delivery, GDS expects annual capex to remain
elevated at Rmb30–50bn, underpinning the buildout required to meet its
expanding order backlog.
•
Earnings outlook remains solid. Overall, we remain optimistic towards
the move-in pace, and expect further acceleration into 2H26E and
2027E. Pricing remains largely stable, with MSR at Rmb1,909, mainly
reflecting a minor decline from contract renewals, offsetting by declining
unit buildout cost, which has declined by ~15% over the past three
years to Rmb20,000/KW. We project the company's backlog version to
accelerate, providing high visibility for multi-year cash flow.
Earnings changes: We lower our 2026E/27E EPS estimates to
Rmb0.10/0.21, from Rmb0.19/0.23, respectively. We expect strong bookings
to be reflected in earnings from late 2027E.
Valuation: We trim our SOTP-based TPs by 4%/4% to US$64.60/HK$54.60,
from US$67.50/HK$57.10, to reflect earnings changes. We maintain our
2026E EV/EBITDA target multiple at 18x on the company's domestic
business given a strong demand upcycle.
Catalysts: Additional key clients/deals that drive backlog and/or enhance
growth visibility; more clarity on imported chip supply.
Investment Thesis and Recommendation
We think GDS is poised to gain from strong demand for high-performance
data centres amid accelerating AI adoption in China, with more new order
commitments raising long-term growth visibility. Active capital recycling
supports balance-sheet strength and scalable growth. Prior GDS research.

GDS Holdings
Key Risks to Investment Thesis
•
Change in relationships with major customers
could adversely affect operations due to high client
concentration.
•
Weaker-than-expected execution and asset management
could lead to more challenging funding environment and
threaten ongoing investments.
•
Potential regulatory change in domestic DC buildout could
affect supply-demand balance and reduce pricing power.
•
Potential sell-off from major shareholders.
Company Description
Founded in 2001, listed in the US in 2016 and then in Hong
Kong in 2020, GDS is a leading developer and operator of
high-performance data centres in China. Operating in China
and Southeast Asia, the company had 618,060sqm of area in
service and 132,235sqm of area under construction by the
end of 2Q25.
