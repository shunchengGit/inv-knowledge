◆ Q1 results: ~9% beat on an EBIT level, driven by beats from 
both Services and Cloud. EPS distorted by equity gains  
◆ Minor ~3% capex hike for FY26e, “significant” increase for

◆ Retain Buy, lifting TP to USD435 (USD385), implying ~18%

Q1 results a 9.2% EBIT beat, EPS distorted by equity gains: Total revenues were 
+21.8% yoy to USD109.9bn and 2.7% ahead vs VA consensus at USD107.0bn. Total 
EBIT was USD39.7bn, +29.7% yoy and 9.2% ahead of consensus at USD36.4bn. At an 
EPS level, diluted EPS was +81.8% yoy to USD5.11 and +87.3% ahead of consensus of 
USD2.73 driven once again by gains in equity securities of USD36.9bn, which we think is 
likely due to Anthropic and/or SpaceX (both private businesses). Removing this impact, 
diluted EPS would have been USD2.76, broadly in-line with consensus.  
◆ Google Services revenues were +16.0% yoy to USD89.6bn and a 1.7% beat vs 
consensus. The growth was driven by strong GCP across both AI solutions and 
enterprise AI infrastructure. GAAP EBIT was +24.2% to USD40.6bn, an 8.0% beat vs 
consensus. This represents an EBIT margin of 45.3%, a record high. We assume 
this quarter is the high for the year but note risk to the upside. 
◆ Google Cloud revenues +63.4% to USD20.0bn and an 8.8% beat vs consensus. 
EBIT was +~200% to USD6.6bn, a 30.9% beat and implying a margin of 32.9%, 
+282bps. The cloud backlog number grew by nearly 2x qoq rising to ~USD460bn, 
from ~USD240bn. Of the backlog, it was noted on the call that the vast majority is for 
the GCP platform with some due to external chip sales expected to impact the 
revenue line from the tail end of FY26e, with a greater impact from FY27e. It was 
also noted that the acquisition of Wiz will lead to lower sequential margins at Google 
Cloud over the remainder of the year by a “low single digit point”. We conservatively 
assume -3% vs Q1 for the remainder.  
Capex guidance hiked for FY26e & 27e: For FY26e, capex guidance has been lifted to 
USD180-190bn from USD175-185bn. However, for FY27e, the commentary is for a 
“significant” increase. We now assume USD250bn for FY27e, +32% yoy, but note the last 
time “significant” increases were guided to it lead to a ~100% increase or USD100bn in 
absolute terms. The company bought back no shares in Q1; thus, we cut our 
expectations going forward to nil, helping to fund this capex increase.  
Remain Buy rated with USD435.00 TP (from USD385.00), implying ~18% upside: 
We update our model for the release, increasing our EBIT expectation
