Sales growth may decelerate but profitability 
could enhance YoY
Sales to decelerate; price raise and higher online mix could support profit
Market questions on sustainability post Q1 beat (see our note) and resulted in 9% share 
price correction. From Q2, we expect SSSG to decelerate, mitigated by increasing online 
sales mix and strong productivity of stores opened or expanded in 2025-26. We estimate 
that NPM for online channel is L-MSD higher than offline channel considering savings on 
rental and depreciation, which more than offset platform commission fee and a 
potentially lower GPM than offline. We introduced a scenario analysis for Laopu 2026 
earnings forecast with three key drivers: 1) online sales mix, 2) same-store volume 
growth, and 3) productivity of new stores. Our base case comes to 55%/ 67% YoY 
growth for revenue/ net profit in 2026E, implying 20-30% growths in Q2-Q426E. Our 
upside/ downside case leads to 131%/ 29% YoY net profit growth.
Channel check update: negative 20-30% SSSG in March and April-to-date
Based on our observations and channel check, we noticed 20-30% SSSG decline in 
March and April-to-date in the Beijing high-base store, which achieved 50% growth in 
CNY holiday season. We attribute the decline to 1) weak gold price, 2) a shortened 
shopping festival (20 days in 2026 March-April vs c40 days in 2025 March-April), and 3) 
inventories that Daigou purchased in Jan-Feb before price raise have not yet been fully 
consumed. Both ticket size and transaction volume have declined. By consumer group, 
we noticed a deeper sales decline among Daigou than ordinary consumers. Demand 
from VIC consumers showed better resiliency than middle class and entry-level 
consumer groups. We view the trend in line with our expectation.
Near-term sector demand shifted by weak gold price
After reaching US$5,300 in early March, gold price retracted to around US$4,700 with 
near-term uncertainties from Middle East situation. This has pressured demand for fixed-
price gold jewelry in the near-term, while supported demand for weight-based gold 
jewelry on a low base. Though we are in the latter stage of gold's bull cycle, UBS view on 
gold price to reach new highs remain unchanged (see Global Precious Metals Outlook 
report). We expect fixed-price gold demand to recover if gold price resumes the upward 
trend and that brands have presence outside of Mainland China to benefit more, 
considering the pricing gap post domestic VAT change.
Valuation: Buy rating; PT of HK$930.0
Trading at 11x 2026E PE on a 6.6% dividend yield, we view risk reward of Laopu 
attractive. Our upside/ downside case suggest that Laopu is trading at 8x -15x 2026E PE. 
Our DCF-derived PT implies 18x 2026E PE on the base case.

drivers: 1) online sales mix, 2) same-store volume growth, and 3) productivity of new 
stores. In Q126, we estimate that 1) online contributed to 20% sales (vs low-teens in 
Q125), 2) SSSG was around 50% where volume per same store was largely flattish, and 
that 3) sales per new store (those opened in 2025) were c60% higher that of same 
stores (vs 30% lower productivity than same stores in 2025). In our upside/ downside 
cases, we assume 1) 30%/ 15% online sales mix in Q2-Q426, 2) same-store volume to 
decline by 20%/60%, and 3) new store productivity of 180%/120%. Our upside case 
implies 113%/ 131% revenue/ NP YoY growth. Our downside case implies 16%/29% 
revenue/ NP YoY growth in 2026E.

Company Description
Opening its first boutique in Beijing in 2009, Laopu was the first gold jewellery company in 
China to promote the concept of heritage gold. It also pioneered embedding diamonds in 
pure gold and introducing pure gold enamel jewellery. Laopu differentiates its products with 
innovative and complex craftsmanship. As of December 2024, Laopu self-operated 36 
boutiques at high-end shopping malls in the mainland's high-tier cities, as well as in Hong 
Kong and Macau.
Valuation Method and Risk Statement
We base our price target on a DCF methodology.
We think key risks facing China's jewellery sector include: 1) a slowdown in China's economy, 
which could dampen consumers' spending on jewellery; 2) lab-grown diamonds could gain 
market share and intensify consumers' price sensitivity; 3) high volatility in gold prices may 
waver consumers’ purchasing decision; and 4) competition from the Shuibei model, which 
sells retail gold jewellery at wholesale prices.
Company-specific downside risks include: 1) competition from other jewellers as consumers 
might not be able to differentiate their products; 2) fixed-price gold being criticised for its 
ambiguous pricing structure; 3) consumers may turn to imitation products at lower prices; 
and 4) the potential stock selling of cornerstone and other IPO/pre-IPO investors after their 
lock-up period.
