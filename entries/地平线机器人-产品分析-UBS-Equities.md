Horizon Robotics
 2026 product launch: transitioning to an auto AI 
agent solutions supplier
Three new products announced at the launch 
Horizon Robotics released three new products at its 2026 launch event: the HSD V1.6; a 
cockpit-driving fusion chip (Starry, 5nm); and the KaKaClaw auto OS. The company also 
plans to launch HSD V2.0 later this year, likely with enhanced human-like AD capability. 
The most noteworthy areas of the event were: 1) the cockpit-driving fusion chip's  
readiness/attractiveness, and a dozen partners; 2) Horizon's transition to an automotive 
AI agent solutions supplier, including OS platform, leveraging  its expertise in SoC, full 
stack AD solutions and the edge AI model. We believe these strategies will enable the 
company to enhance its position in the AD SoC and cockpit solutions market, and we 
expect a positive reaction from the market post the event. 
Starry  likely a new growth driver from 2027E
Horizon launched the Starry 6P/H cockpit-driving fusion  SoC (TSMC 5nm), powered by 
20/14 core CPUs  and supporting 650 TOPS/500TOPS BPU computing power (vs. J6P 
560TOPS). According to Horizon, compared with peers the Starry 6P can achieve c1.4x 
cockpit performance, 10-19x AD performance and 2.4x edge AI model processing 
speed. As per management, the Starry 6 series' major benefits include: 1) significant cost 
savings (Rmb1,500-4,000 per vehicle), including a memory cost reduction of Rmb2,000-
3,000 by saving 20-24GB of DDR; 2) a shortened OEM intelligent solution design cycle 
to eight months (18 months previously) via system-level integration; and 3) interactive 
cockpit intelligence functions along with reliable AD solutions. Horizon expects  Starry's 
SOP in Q326, with Chery being its first  partner. UBS expects memory prices to continue 
to rise up to Q4227E, with major memory makers’ capacity being prioritised to support 
IDC customers. We expect the Starry solution to be attractive to auto customers.
Vertical integration and expansion into an AI agent
Horizon released its auto OS platform, KaKaClaw. This agentic OS  enables: 1) 
continuous planning; 2) interactive cockpit  activities such as playing games and 
greetings; and 3) full scenario adaptive capabilities. The company said KaKaClaw is 
compatible with non-Horizon SoCs. Management expects its "HSD+KaKaClaw" to be a 
significant auto intelligence solution in China, benchmarking "FSD+Grok". Although 
KaKaClaw is new and not mature, we believe it will facilitate Horizon's transformation 
into an auto AI agent solutions supplier and increase user stickiness.
Valuation: reiterate price target of HK$13.20 and Buy rating
We have not included potential earnings upside from the new product launch into our 
model, but we will revisit our forecasts. Our price target is based on DCF, assuming a 
WACC of 9.5%.

Company Description
Horizon Robotics is a leading provider of advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and 
autonomous driving (AD) solutions, primarily focused on the PV market. It develops 
proprietary software and hardware technologies, including algorithms, specialized brain 
processing units (BPUs) and development tools. Horizon Robotics operates as a tier 2 supplier 
providing integrated solutions to OEMs and tier 1 suppliers. It is a key player in China's ADAS 
chip market, competing with global leaders and leveraging software-hardware co-
optimzation and an open platform business model.
Valuation Method and Risk Statement
Our price target for Horizon Robotics is based on DCF methodology.
Downside risks include order intake missing expectations, lower EV shipments and rising 
competition among vendors.
