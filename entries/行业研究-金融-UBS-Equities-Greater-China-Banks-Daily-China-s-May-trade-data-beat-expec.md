China’s May trade data beat expectations on strong AI-related demand
Caixin, Reuters, Jun 9: According to Custom data released, China’s exports in May 2026 
totaled USD 376.78bn, hitting a 19.4% YoY growth, up from the 14.1% growth in April 
and outperforming the expected 15% in a Reuters poll. The import value reported USD 
271.35bn, up 27.4% YoY, also accelerating from April’s 25.3% growth and beating 
Reuters’ expectation of 25% YoY. The trade surplus in May recorded USD105.3bn, 
increasing by USD 20.61bn MoM, hitting a same-period record high. The strong exports 
were fueled by robust demand for AI-related products, with integrated circuits and 
automatic data processing equipment leading the growth (+110.9% and +66.1% YoY 
in May). Exports to major partners mostly accelerated, including the U.S. (up 35.4% YoY 
on a low base), ASEAN (+24.3%), Japan (+10.9%), Africa (+18.6%), Russia (+35.8%), 
and especially South Korea (+42.1%), while growth to the EU (+7.6%) and Latin 
America (+6%) slowed. 
China’s Tier-1 city rents rise for a third straight month
STCN, Jun 9: According to CREIS data, the average rent for ordinary residential 
properties in China’s Tier-1 cities rose by an average of 0.16% MoM in May, marking the 
third consecutive month of MoM increase (+0.34%/+0.26% MoM in Mar/ Apr), mainly 
driven by employment-related demand. Among the four Tier-1 cities, Shanghai, Beijing 
and Shenzhen showed impressive consecutive growth in rent prices since March 2026, 
while Guangzhou had a slight decline in May (-0.25%). In particular, Shanghai took the 
lead with its rent growth staying above 0.5% MoM for both April and May. 
Regulator pledges to curb disorderly competition, bolster institutions' capital, 
and tighten risk control
Caixin, Jun 9: On the first meeting of NFRA after its new chief Ding Xiangqun took 
office, the agency announced its commitment to several priorities. The meeting called 
for deeply rectifying disorderly competition in finance sector, shifting the sector from 
chasing speed and scale toward quality and efficiency; firmly advancing "quantity 
reduction and quality improvement" for small and medium-sized institutions; and 
crucially, broadening channels to replenish capital to strengthen institutions' capital base 
and sustainability. In parallel, the NFRA reiterated the urgency to prevent financial risks, 
with a particular focus on ensuring that local small and mid-sized financial institutions 
avoid major defaults. Other priorities included reinforcing the whitelist mechanism for 
unfinished housing projects, and supporting the restructuring and transition of LGFVs to 
help contain local government debt risks.  
Other key news
(1) Guangzhou’s revised housing provident fund loan rules took effect on June 9, 
expanding eligible loan categories, easing application requirements for foreign passport 
holders, and adjusting the formula used to calculate loan amounts. The new measures 
are aimed at improving policy accessibility and better supporting home purchase 
demand, and will remain valid for five years. (STCN, Jun 9)
(2) Village bank' reform and de-risking are advancing. Per the NAFR's database, as of Jun 
1, 97 village banks had appeared on the "institutional exit list" this year. (Sina, Jun 9)

Valuation Method and Risk Statement
We derive our price targets for H-share China banks based on a three-stage dividend discount 
model (DDM). We derive our price targets for A-share China banks based on P/B to ROE 
valuation methodology.
We think major risks to China banks include: 1) deterioration in asset quality, underpinned by 
a weaker macro environment and domestic property market activity; 2) regulatory risks 
related to capital, liquidity and off-balance-sheet activities of banks; 3) deterioration in 
funding structure and balance-sheet liquidity positions, driven by a potential rollover of loans 
and a lengthening of asset durations; and 4) liberalisation of interest rates in the medium 
term and the consequent pressure on bank profitability.
