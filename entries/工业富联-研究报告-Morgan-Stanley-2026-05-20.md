Foxconn Industrial Internet Co. Ltd.  | Asia Pacific
AI Servers Continue to Lead 
2Q26 Growth
AI server the major growth driver: Management indicated 2Q26e revenue growth 
at >15% YoY across the board, with AI server rack shipments a major driver and 
GB300 still being a majority of shipments. FII sees consumer electronics recovery in 
2H26, while AI business should show rapid growth throughout the year. As Vera 
Rubin-related contribution increases, management expects the overall gross margin 
structure to improve. 
Vera Rubin time-line intact: FII sees no delay for the Vera Rubin NVL72 rack and is 
currently preparing related capacity. Management targets mass production for VR 
racks by the end of 3Q26, with production ramp-up expected in 4Q26. The company 
is confident it can grow its supply share as rack complexity and delivery 
requirements are higher in next generation product offerings.
CPO switch adoption will likely start to grow this year and FII expects to enter mass 
production (L10 assembly) in 3Q26 with >10,000 units this year. This business is 
margin-accretive, with much higher value added compared to traditional network 
switch assembly work.

Key Takeaways
Management indicated 2Q26e revenue growth at >15% YoY across the board, 
with AI server rack shipments a major driver.
Mechanical parts for new smartphone model and CPO switches have entered 
preparation for mass production, with both scheduled for mass production in 
3Q26.
800G above switch, LEO satellite, CPO switch and foldable smartphones are 
major business drivers, in addition to Vera Rubin POD introduction. 
We remain OW post its 1Q26 earnings call, with a clear product pipeline through 
year end.
