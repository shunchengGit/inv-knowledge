On an estimated ~40% unit CAGR for the China indigenous AI chip fabless sector 
over 2026-30, we believe tier-2 suppliers with: 1) secured foundry supply, and 2) 
potential cloud service provider (CSP) client breakthrough could outperform. We 
initiate coverage of Iluvatar CoreX with an OW rating (link), as we believe the 
company is less constrained by supply and could benefit from the prefill/decode 
(P/D) disaggregation trend. We also like MetaX (link), which adopts a fully 
localized supply chain and benefits from China government-sponsored AI 
applications. However, given its stretched valuation, we recommend waiting for a 
better entry point and we initiate coverage with a Neutral rating. 
• Indigenous AI chip demand set to soar. Based on our token assumptions, with 
the added uncertainties of US AI chip supply, we expect China indigenous AI 
chip demand (units) to hit a c.40% 5-year CAGR by 2030. With superior single 
chip performance and local foundry capacity, we believe tier-1 suppliers could 
be imminent substitutes for US AI chips, while select tier-2 suppliers could 
offer higher sales growth, with an elaborate product strategy and 
inference/application focus. 
• Supply is a key bottleneck. Global advanced foundry services have been 
significantly disrupted since 1H25 with US export controls/regulations. The 
impact on tier-2 AI chip fabless has been more severe with limited local foundry 
capacity for advanced node. Most tier-2 suppliers have had to leverage existing 
inventory to fulfill demand in the next 6-12 months; product and supply chain 
strategy are key factors for longer-term sales growth, in our view. 
• CSP client breakthrough a pivotal driver. Tier-2 suppliers are more focused 
on government-sponsored AI projects currently, such as local government AIDC 
and smart city. As top CSPs have not yet deployed tier-2 AI chips substantially, 
design-wins for CSP clients could drive higher growth potential for select tier-2 
players, in our view. 
• Iluvatar CoreX our top pick. With efficient architecture design, we believe 
Iluvatar CoreX can not only secure chip supply, but also benefit from the P/D 
disaggregation trend and break into the supply chain of the top CSPs. MetaX is 
better positioned in government-sponsored projects, thanks to its sufficient 
inventory and fully localized supply chain. The sector is currently trading at 27x 
2027E P/S on average, based on consensus estimates. We initiate coverage of 
Iluvator CoreX at OW with a Jun-27 PT of HK$620 (based on 20x forward P/S), 
and we initiate coverage of MetaX at Neutral with a Jun-27 PT of Rmb550 
(based on 30x forward P/S).

Investment Thesis 
Indigenous AI chip demand is set to ramp
Post the crowded IPOs of Chinese AI chip companies over the past 3-4 months, we 
believe we are now in the phase of commercialization or industrialization of 
domestic GPUs or AI ASICs, given the intensive expected investment in AI 
infrastructure. 
We believe soaring token consumption in China is a substantial demand driver for 
indigenous AI chips. Our JPM Internet team is forecasting (link) China token 
consumption to show an approximately 330% CAGR over 2025-30, driven by: 1) 
penetration rises across both consumer users and enterprise workloads, and 2) use case 
expansion from conversational AI toward agentic and multimodal output (increasing 
tokens per session). 
We believe the rapidly rising token consumption is largely driven by inference, and 
the incremental demand from training should be marginal (increasing parameters and 
multimodal as key catalysts). Based on our token demand forecasts, we derive our 
chip demand forecasts suggesting a ~40% CAGR for AI chip unit demand over 
2026-30E. 
Such AI chip unit demand is based on our throughput assumption per processor (i.e. 
tokens per second per TFLOPS to increase from 1.0 in 2025 to 3.3 in 2030), with a 
specific service level for inference (e.g. 20 tokens/second output with a batch size of 
128 and KV Cache Length of 4,096). Meanwhile, the other key underlying 
assumptions include: 1) single compute die per AI chip, 2) steady compute die 
performance improvement in coming years, and 3) an increasing utilization rate of 
China AIDC capacity.

Two types of indigenous AI chip fabless
We categorize China indigenous AI chip fabless companies into two groups: 
Tier-1 suppliers: The revenue size is approaching ~Rmb10bn in 2026E, with 
existing sales exposed to China’s top CSPs (i.e. Alibaba, Tencent and Bytedance). 
We believe there are 4 players in tier-1: Huawei, Cambricon, Kunlunxin, and T-
Head. 
Tier-1 suppliers hold some advantages: 1) higher single chip performance (Huawei, 
Cambricon); 2) capacity support from local/global foundries (Huawei, Cambricon, 
Kunlunxin); and 3) relationships with top CSPs (Kunlunxin, T-head). With the 
uncertainties of US AI chip supply, tier-1 suppliers are set to substitute US chips 
imminently, in our view. 
Tier-2 suppliers: The revenue base is relatively low for tier-2 suppliers, and they 
have limited exposure to China top CSPs. Companies in this group include: Biren, 
MetaX, Iluvatar CoreX, Moore Threads and Enflame.

Supply is the key bottleneck, in the short to mid term
Given Chinese AI chip companies only have limited access to advanced capacity 
overseas, especially under certain transistor density or technology nodes, they have 
no choice but to transfer chip production to local leading-edge foundries such as 
SMIC (0981 HK) or at least to prepare indigenous supply chain locally. However, 
the existing advanced capacity for GPU/ AI ASICs has been largely occupied by 
Huawei Ascend and Cambricon, based on our supply chain checks. 
Due to the difficulty of building leading-edge capacity and inferior production yields, 
we believe the availability of wafer fabrication would be the key bottleneck at the 
current stage. Also, a lack of critical equipment such as Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) 
lithography system, inspection and metrology caused the longer learning curve and 
then slower capacity ramp-up; our supply chain checks suggest tier-2 players were 
only able to initiate the tape-in process at leading-edge tech node by late 2025, hence 
significant shipments of local-produced chips should be expected from 2H26 
onwards. 
Considering both exponential (inference) demand and likely restrained overseas 
supply, we expect domestic AI computing chips to remain a seller's market for the 
next 6 to 18 months, under a progressive release of fab capacity locally (assumed). 
With that said, we believe chip players with a high inventory buffer could be 
preferred by customers in the near term. Meanwhile, we have observed that many 
companies have prepared high inventory (comparable to revenue) to ensure smooth 
and timely delivery to customers.
