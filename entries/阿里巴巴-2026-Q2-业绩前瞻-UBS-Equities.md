We estimate revenue flattish YoY at Rmb237bn for 4QFY26E. By segment: 1) China e-

5% YoY in Mar Q (Dec's +1%), with growth accelerating in Jan-Feb due to the delayed 
winter and CNY seasonality before normalizing into Mar. On CMR, we revised down our 
growth forecast to +1% YoY (from prior +6%), given the introduction of merchant 
subsidies in Mar Q (accounted as contra revenue, and lower S&M expense, hence no net 
EBITA impact; similar to PDD's smart coupons). This is mainly targeted at brands, likely 
with higher GMV and CMR medium-term growth potential. Excluding the subsidies 
impact, CMR should have remained on track at 6-7% growth. Down to earnings, we 
forecast segment EBITA to be at Rmb26bn, assuming TTG EBITA flattish YoY. On quick 
commerce, we estimate cRmb15bn loss (cons Rmb18bn) for Mar Q, with mgmt 
committed to reduce segment loss by half each year in FY27E and FY28E, with goal of 
achieving profitability by FY29E.
2) Cloud: We expect revenue at +40% YoY (Dec Q's +36%), with acceleration driven by 
continued robust external demand. We expect EBITA margin  to be steady at 9%.
3) International commerce (AIDC): We expect revenue at +5% YoY. We expect EBITA 
loss  to narrow to  Rmb0.4bn (4QFY25's Rmb3.6bn loss) amid more prudent expansion 
and AliExpress' improving UE. 
4) All others: We expect EBITA loss to widen to Rmb20bn (Dec Q's Rmb9.8bn). This is 
driven by increasing computing needs for Qwen model training (recent launch of new 
flagship LLM Qwen3.6-Plus and video generation model Happy Horse), CNY campaign 
for Qwen ToC app (estimated at Rmb5bn+), and other BABA's native apps (Quark, 
Amap, etc). Mgmt highlighted that BABA is currently in an investment phase, marked by 
frequent model, AI feature and native app launches, which have driven solid growth 
momentum, underpinning potential long-term upside in AI opportunities. Overall, we 
estimate adj. EBITA at Rmb5.9bn (cons Rmb15.6bn), -82% YoY factoring in the 
above.

EPS cut  from increased AI investment: Incorporating higher AI-related expenditures 
(reflected within the All Others corporate segment) while accounting for accelerated 
loss reduction in quick commerce, we revise FY26-28E adj. EPS downward by 8-21%.

commerce to AI, a sector with a higher growth potential. Longer term, BABA 
continues to distinguish itself as a comprehensive and direct AI leader, supported by its 
full stack industry-leading GPU capabilities, LLM advancements, and Cloud offerings. 
Following the earnings adjustments, BABA trades at 20x FY27E P/E, positioning it at 
the upper end of the China internet sector and approaching levels among the 
US Mag 7 peers. With near-term EPS likely to remain under pressure due to challenges 
in e-commerce (macroeconomic headwinds and competition) and elevated AI 
investments, its stock performance is likely to be  driven  more by market sentiment and 
the outlook for China’s AI sector, rather than EPS upgrades. We adjust our SOTP-based 
PT to US$170/HK$166 (previously US$190/185) to reflect higher AI investment and 
reiterate our Buy rating.

Company Description
Alibaba Group is a global internet company with six business groups. Its core business is 
Taobao Tmall Commerce, which includes Taobao, Tmall and other e-commerce platforms and 
offline infrastructure in China. Local services include on-demand food and grocery delivery, 
maps and location-based services. Cainiao Smart Logistics provides logistics support in China 
and overseas. In addition, Alibaba has cloud, digital entertainment & media and international 
commerce groups.
Valuation Method and Risk Statement
Our price target is based on SOTP.
We believe the key risks for Alibaba are: 1) regulatory changes, particularly for data usage and 
online content; 2) Chinese and global macroeconomic headwinds; 3) competitive pressure 
from traditional offline retailers; 4) interruptions of information technology and systems; 5) 
short-term profitability pressure from long-term investment; 6) execution and management 
complexity resulting from its multiple platforms; 7) corporate governance – significant voting 
control by the Alibaba Partnership; and 8) the loss of the services of founder Jack Ma.
