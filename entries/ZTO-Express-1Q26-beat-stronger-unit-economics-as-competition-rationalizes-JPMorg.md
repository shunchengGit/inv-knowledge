ZTO reported 1Q26 results and held a briefing this morning; the quarter 
came in as a beat, driven by better-than-expected ASP and mix, while unit-
cost improvements also surprised positively as AI/digitalization continued 
to translate into measurable efficiency gains. Briefing tone stayed 
constructive on the durability of anti-involution and the improving 
competitive landscape, even as industry volume growth is clearly 
softening (recent April datapoints point to further deceleration), which 
likely anchors full-year volume outcome toward the low end of the 10–
13% guidance range. Net-net, the setup remains supportive for pricing 
discipline, margin progression and share gains for scaled leaders, with 
ZTO showing tangible evidence in 1Q26 via share lift (c.+1.2ppt) and 
pricing uplift (+Rmb0.11/parcel) alongside Rmb0.06/parcel transit unit-
cost reduction.
Stock view: Stock reaction post 1Q26 result was negative yesterday 
(ZTO-H -3% vs. HSC flattish), driven by Alibaba continuing to trim its 
stake and the termination of the investor rights agreement; we see this as a 
sentiment-driven overhang rather than a fundamental deterioration, and 
view dips as buying opportunities. We keep OW and keep the name as a 
top pick in logistics alongside JD Logistics, given the company’s ability 
to grow faster than industry, hold pricing above the floor in a more rational 
regime, and keep compounding structural cost advantages through 
technology and network execution.
Briefing takeaways – Confidence in both market share 
gains and margin improvement sets the name apart 
heading into 2026 compared to prior years
• Full-year guidance stays unchanged, but the path skews toward the 
low end on volume while profitability momentum is poised to look 
stronger in 2Q on base effects. Management reiterated FY26 parcel 
volume guidance unchanged at 10–13% Y/Y (42.37–43.52B). However, 
the briefing and callback both pointed to industry growth decelerating 
further—April data already soft, and May tracking low single digits—so 
volume is likely to trend toward the lower end of the range, with ZTO 
still running ahead of the industry. The profitability setup into 2Q is 
more constructive than the volume headline: callback flagged a low base 
in 2Q25 (peak price war) and overseas dividend tax item (Rmb166MM) 
in 2Q25, with operating profit growth poised to print strong Y/Y 
(c.40%), while bottom-line outcome depends on non-op items.
• Anti-involution is still the anchor for pricing and competitive sanity 
even as industry growth cools. The briefing reinforced that anti-
involution remains in force post-CNY, with stronger enforcement in 
production-heavy provinces and tightening in laggard regions,

• Retail and reverse logistics are now meaningful strategic levers, not 
side bets, and they are lifting mix, pricing power and share. 
Management highlighted retail volume +65% Y/Y, with 1Q daily retail 
volumes at 9.7MM and an implied improving run-rate in 2Q, alongside 
c.+1.4ppt increase in market presence and overall market share up 
c.+1.2ppt in 1Q26. Ecommerce platforms remain efficiency-led with 
Douyin a major contributor (ZTO’s daily volume from Douyin is 
c.7MM, c.50% share). This mix matters for unit economics: ASP uplift 
in 1Q (+Rmb0.11) was driven by Key Accounts (+Rmb0.18) while 
volume incentives (-Rmb0.09) partially offset, and management also 
flagged reverse parcels remain more profitable than standard e-
commerce parcels even as reverse ASP trends below Rmb5 and could 
drift lower with platform choices and tenders.
• AI and digitalization are moving from cost-out tools to operating-
system upgrades, supporting both cost discipline and service quality 
differentiation. Management framed AI as deeply embedded across the 
chain: transport optimization and route design, digitized load-factor 
standards and incentives, driver cost-saving incentives, and standardized 
fleet O&M cost models. The tangible unit-economics read-through is 
strong in 1Q: transportation cost per parcel was Rmb0.37 (down 
Rmb0.04 Y/Y) and sorting cost was Rmb0.25 (down Rmb0.02 Y/Y). 
Management also guided that full-year unit transit cost is poised to 
decline by a further Rmb0.03. Beyond cost, AI tools are being used to 
improve operational control and decision velocity, supporting both 
service reliability and lower manual intervention.
• Cost swings like diesel and social security are treated as manageable, 
with price discipline and network stability as the offset. On diesel 
price, management noted March price hikes for pass-throughs, but noted 
April saw easing; the key message is that anti-involution-related price 
lifts have largely covered fuel inflation, and 2Q diesel price volatility is 
unlikely to be a major swing factor for network cost. On social security, 
management welcomed faster rollout despite near-term unit-cost 
pressure, viewing it as consistent with anti-involution’s direction; longer 
term, a more stable labor setup lowers churn and supports last-mile 
service quality. This matters strategically because ZTO is prioritizing 
network capability upgrades rather than extracting value from outlets for

Results review – 1Q26 beat was driven by pricing/mix upside and faster-than-expected 
efficiency gains
• Revenue and earnings beat consensus on better ASP, faster 
efficiency gains, and a less supportive non-op base in 1Q25. 1Q26 
revenue grew +22.0% Y/Y to Rmb13.28B, beating both our estimate 
(Rmb12.53B) and BBG (Rmb12.60B) by c5%. Parcel volume growth 
was +13.2% Y/Y, in line with our expectation and materially ahead of 
industry growth (5.8%), translating into market share lift of c.+1.2ppt. 
The key upside driver was pricing/mix: management flagged price hikes 
of +8% Y/Y and ASP up +Rmb0.11, driven by +Rmb0.18 from KA mix, 
partly offset by -Rmb0.09 from volume incentives. Profitability was 
more nuanced on headline margins—GPM dipped 0.3ppt to 24.4% and 
non-GAAP net margin fell 2.8ppt to 17.9%—but adjusted net profit still 
rose +5.2% Y/Y to Rmb2.38B (vs. guidance for flat), and the “quality of 
beat” looks better once adjusting for non-op: excluding the reduced 
government subsidy versus 1Q25, underlying earnings growth ran closer 
to +22%.
• Unit economics improved meaningfully, with transport and transit

with heavier use of automation, digitized management tools, load-factor 
optimization, route design, and tighter driver and fleet cost controls.

ZTO Express (Cayman) Inc. - H (Overweight; Price Target: HK$225.00)
Investment Thesis 
ZTO Express is China’s largest express parcel provider, with an approximate 20% market share (as of FY25), and is the 
most profitable entity among the Tongda players, which include YTO, STO, Yunda and J&T. ZTO’s competitive edge is 
particularly evident in its strong positioning on ecommerce platforms including Taobao and Douyin, attributed to its 
superior service quality and most efficient, well-established logistics infrastructure network. ZTO continues to balance 
market share retention with profitability as China’s anti-involution policy continues.
Valuation
Our Jun-27 H-share PT of HK$225 is based on our ZTO-US DCF-based Jun-27 PT of US$29, a USD/HKD exchange rate 
of 7.8 and a conversion ratio of 1:1.
Risks to Rating and Price Target
Downside risks to our rating and price target include: (1) a weaker-than-anticipated macroeconomic environment, which 
could negatively impact our volume projections and hinder growth; and 2) an unforeseen increase in oil prices, which could 
elevate operational costs and challenge our cost forecasts.
