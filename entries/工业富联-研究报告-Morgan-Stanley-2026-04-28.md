Foxconn Industrial Internet Co. Ltd.  | Asia Pacific
1Q26 Operating Profit Beat
1Q26  details: Revenue was Rmb251bn, down 16% QoQ but up 57% YoY, driven by 
AI-related shipment strength, even though some newly added orders from CSP 
clients are on consignment booking; previously, they were mainly buy-and-sell.
Gross margin and operating margin continued to trend higher, to 7.9% and 6.2%, 
respectively.  
Operating profit thus beat our estimate and consensus by 7% and 14%, respectively. 
Non-operating losses  eroded core profit and resulted in net profit of Rmb10,595mn 
or EPS of Rmb0.53, 17% below our estimates.
• Cloud equipment: Revenue doubled YoY. Within this, AI GPU rack shipment 
volume increased 3.8x YoY and AI ASIC server shipments also grew 3.2x YoY.
• Telecom and networking equipment: 800G and above high speed switch 
shipment units rose 1.6x YoY, up 46% QoQ. CPO switches also started 
sampling with key customers. 
• Inventory: Reached Rmb167.3bn in 1Q26, up 11% QoQ and 39% YoY, with 
turnover days down to 52, vs. 64 in 4Q25 and 59 a year ago.
• Operating cash flow: Stayed  pisitive and  increased to Rmb25bn in 1Q26.

Valuation Methodology and Risks
 Foxconn Industrial Internet Co. Ltd. (601138.SS)  
Base case, derived from a multi-stage residual income (RI) valuation model. Key assumptions:
n A 10% cost of equity, in line with our tech hardware coverage universe.
n A medium-term growth rate of 18%, reflecting the benefits of its ongoing investment, 
enriched solution offerings and multi-year AI demand opportunity.
n A terminal growth rate of 5%.
 Risks to Upside  
n Faster project win progress in IIoT solutions
n Lower competition in its manufacturing business
n Faster-than-expected 5G and data center demand
n Stronger macro outlook
 Risks to Downside  
n Slow project win progress in IIoT solutions
n Increased competition in its manufacturing business
n Lower-than-expected 5G and data center demand
