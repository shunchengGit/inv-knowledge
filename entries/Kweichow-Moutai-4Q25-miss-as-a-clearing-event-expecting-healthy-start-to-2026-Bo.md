4Q25 miss as a clearing event 
We trim 2026/27E EPS by -2%/-4% due to Moutai’s 4Q miss, while maintaining PO of 
RMB1630 as we factor in its recent price hike (see our prior note). We highlight 4 result 
takeaways.  1) Revenue/NPAT was -1%/-4.5% in 2025 (or -19%/-30% in 4Q25), 2%/5% 
below BofA estimates and VA consensus. 4Q miss was due to destocking before the 
end‑2025 channel reform (which has since proven successful), soft demand and CNY 
shipment phasing.  2) We regard 4Q (the first quarterly EPS decline since 2015) as a 
clearing event, which should pave the way for a likely 2026 recovery, evidenced by sell-
out growth in 1Q26, per our channel checks. Moutai’s 4Q should also set the tone for the 
baijiu sector with broad-based declines in 4Q25/1Q26. 3) Moutai has not offered 2026 
guide in the filing (an unusual move). However, we expect mid-single digit growth given 
a lower base from its 4Q miss, a solid start to 2026 and c.5% GDP growth target of 
Guizhou (its home province). 4) Dividend payout was 79% in 2025 (vs.75% in 2024), and 
dividend dollars were +0.6% YoY despite EPS declines, offering downside protection.  
1Q result is key in the near term; maintain Buy 
1Q will be key in the near term, and we remain constructive. 1) We expect the industry 
to see easier comps ahead given the lap of anti-graft campaigns from May, and P&L 
cleanup from 2H. 2) Moutai enjoys better visibility, while other large-cap peers all face 
their own issues. 3) Moutai offers a 3.6% dividend yield, or c.4% total shareholder yield, 
including share buyback, which appears attractive in a low-interest-rate environment. 
Results highlight 
1) 2025. Moutai liquor’s sales were +0.4%, with volume +0.7% to 46.8k tons. Series 
liquor sales were -9.8%. DTC was +13% (or 50% of sales). 2) 4Q25. Revenue/NPAT was -
19.3%/-30.3%. Advance payment was +3% QoQ, or -17% YoY. DTC was +27% YoY, and 
traditional channel were -58% YoY. GPM was -459bp to 74.7%. S&D/sales was +399bp to 
6.7%, and G&A/sales was +392bp to 13.8%. Hence, OPM was -843bp to 61.5%.

Company Description 
With over 800 years of history, Moutai is the largest baijiu 
company in China, with over 50% volume share in 
premium-end baijiu. Its core brand, Moutai (including 53-, 
43- and 38-proof), contributes 91% of revenue. The 
remaining sales come from other series, represented by 
Moutai Prince, Ying Bin and Lai Mou. It is the most well-
known baijiu brand globally and is positioned at the 
premium end of the market with the highest ASP and gross 
margin. The firm was listed in SHEx in August 27, 2001. 
Investment Rationale 
We rate Moutai Buy. The company enjoys indisputable 
industry leadership, brand strength and scarcity value, 
which have led to much more EPS buffers, a smoother 
performance cycle amid the downturn and healthier 
channel dynamics.
