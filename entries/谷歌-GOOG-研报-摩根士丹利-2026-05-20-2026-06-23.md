Sparking the Agentic Torch
I/O ’26 showcased new, more efficient models, and agentic 
offerings across commerce, travel and daily life (Spark). Some 
products will leverage GOOGL’s full distribution, while most will 
sit behind subscription paywalls (for now)...but agentic sparks 
are set to fly with GOOGL lighting the torch.
 Agentic Sparks to Start Flying: GOOGL introduced its personal agent, known as 
Gemini Spark...which will roll out across the Gemini app (now 900M+ MAUs), 
Search, Gmail, Google Calendar, Google Drive and other GOOGL apps. In addition to 
GOOGL's owned and operated distribution surfaces, Gemini Spark will be 
integrating with 3P apps via MCP (an open standard that allows AI agents to 
securely connect with external apps and services), including DASH/UBER/LYFT/
CART, as well as BKNG/EXPE (more on that below). Gemini Spark will be available 
for Google's top tier AI Ultra plan starting in Beta next week and will be available 
directly within Chrome later this summer.
New, Faster and More Efficient Models: GOOGL announced Gemini 3.5 Flash, a 
new, notably faster version of the Gemini model, with Gemini 3.5 Pro set to release 
next month (see here for the model release blog post and benchmark summary). 
GOOGL Gemini also continues to improve compute cost efficiency with Gemini 3.5 
Flash API pricing of $1.50/$9.00 per million input/output tokens…which is 
70%/70% lower than competitors’ leading models. We see GOOGL’s ability to 
continue building more efficient and faster models as an important driver of 
adoption and competitive differentiation. 
GOOGL Leverages Distribution and Lower Pricing on Subscription Tiers: While 
many new/early agentic products from today (discussed below) are only available to 
subscribers in the near-term 1) multiple new products, including Gemini Flash 3.5, 
Intelligent Search Box,  and Universal Cart are being deployed to the full GOOGL 
user base (leveraging their leading distribution) and 2) GOOGL reduced the pricing 
of its premium Ultra subscription tier by 20%  (down to $200/mo from $250/mo 
previously) and introduced a new, intermediate tier for $100/mo. This again speaks 
to GOOGL’s ability to reduce inference cost and leverage its distribution to make it 
more difficult to compete against.
New World and Video Diffusion Models: GOOGL also released a new multi-modal 
world model, Gemini Omni...which is set to lead to more differentiated video 
creative capabilities (like Nano Banana did for image creation). With Omni, users can 
combine images, audio, video and text as input to generate high-quality videos 
grounded in Gemini's real-world knowledge. Over time we see these models driving 
a multitude of engagement and monetization opportunities including: video creative 
for enterprises/SMBs/creators/influencers/users, gaming/interactive media creation, 
advertising creative for more personalized, custom ads and more.
