Price Hikes for Feitian Moutai
Moutai announced that  it will raise ex-factory prices of its core product 53% vol 
Feitian Moutai. The new factory price for distributors would be Rmb1,269/btl 
(previously Rmb1,169/btl at distributor channels) and Rmb1,539/btl on its direct 
channels (previously Rmb1,499/btl).
Beyond Feitian's price hike, Moutai also: 1) increased its direct sales composition; 2) 
reformed non-Feitian high-end product distributor fee structure; and 3) lowered ex-
factory price of Moutai 1935 and non-Feitian high-end SKUs; as part of company's 
market-oriented pricing actions this year.
Our take:  The price hike plan should be EPS accretive for Moutai. Based on our 
understanding, the price hike should reflect Moutai's gauge of "real demand" for 
Feitian, especially after it was launched on iMoutai platform.  We expect wholesale 
prices to rebound in the near term, reacting to the price hikes, but eventually 
demand-supply will be the key factor to decide wholesale prices. The higher price 
for Feitian Moutai could provide some buffer to possible volume cuts on non-Feitain 
Moutai SKUs, in our view.

Key Takeaways
Moutai announced  price hikes for its core product Feitian Moutai from March 31, 
2026. The previous ex-factory price hike was on November 1, 2023.  
 Price increases: 1) by Rmb100/bottle for  ex-factory price to distributors to 
Rmb1269/bottle; 2) by Rmb40/bottle for the  direct retail price to Rmb1539/
bottle.  
 This reflects Moutai's market-oriented approach for pricing and channel 
management since 2026, in our view, after the price cut for  non-Feitian SKUs...
 ...And the initiation of "concession model" for distributors to distribute non-Feitian 
high-end SKUs with 5% concession rate. 
We estimate the price hikes to contribute ~3-4% to 2026-27 EPS without 
considering further volume changes and  to support wholesale price in the short 
term.
