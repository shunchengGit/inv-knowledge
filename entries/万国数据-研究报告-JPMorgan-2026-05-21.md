Ongoing robust order wins with improving visibility over the next few 
years amid strong AI demand: GDS reiterated its 2026 guidance 
of 500MW of new orders, and indicated potential upside given the 340MW of 
new orders year-to-date (130+MW new booking from Hyperscaler with 
77MW in Shaoguan, Guangdong and 61MW in Ulanqab, Inner Mongolia in 
2Q26). Supported by strong AI-driven data center demand and improving 
availability of domestic AI chips, management now guides for 500-800MW of 
new bookings annually over the next three years, with additional upside if 
foreign AI chips become available in China. Management also noted that new 
bookings plus reservation commitments exceed 1GW year-to-date 
(versus 200MW of new orders and 500MW+ of MOUs as of mid-March), and 
that the elevated reservation pipeline has a high probability of converting into 
follow-on orders within 1-2 years.
•
Strong bookings set up a doubling of move-ins in 2027: GDS reported 16k 
sqm of move-ins in 1Q26, down 12% QoQ, and expects move-ins to decline 
further in 2Q26. Management expects move-ins to rebound to around 20k sqm 
per quarter in 2H26, implying total 2026 move-ins of 70k+ sqm. Given a 
typical 6-8 quarter conversion cycle, management expects most orders signed 
in 2026 to translate into move-ins in 2H27, with some carrying into 2028. 
Overall, management guided move-ins to grow at least 25% HoH in 2H26 and 
to double YoY in 2027,which should drive 2027 revenue growth to exceed 
2026 despite an ongoing monthly service revenue (MSR) downtick.
•
2026 capex guidance unchanged; secured land bank expands to 
~4GW: GDS maintained its 2026 capex guidance of RMB 9bn, with the bulk 
of spending expected to be deployed over the remaining three quarters of the 
year (2Q26-4Q26). The company also increased its secured land bank to 
~4GW (from ~3GW that management highlighted on the 4Q25 earnings call) 
and initiated ~400MW of new construction over the past 15 months. To support 
its 500-800MW annual new booking target, management expects total capex 
of ~RMB 40bn (at the mid-point of RMB 30-50bn capex guidance) over the 
next three years and believes around 60% of this could be funded with new 
debt.
•
Gross profit yield remains at 10-11%; stable new-business pricing: While 
management said pricing for incremental AI-driven demand remains stable, 
GDS reported an ~4% YoY decline in monthly service revenue 
(MSR) in 1Q26 and expects MSR to remain on a downward trajectory over the 
next 1-2 years. Despite the declining MSR, management indicated that the 
current adjusted gross profit yield is ~11%, and expects it to remain within 
the 10-11% range, even as new-business volumes ramp and development costs 
also drop. Management noted that development costs for new business are 
about US$3mn per MW, down 15% over the past three years, driven mainly by 
higher power density and scale benefits.
