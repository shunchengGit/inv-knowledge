Earnings outlook improved, again. Following earnings upgrades post its 3Q25 
results and 4Q25 results,  we raise forecasts the third time  post its 1Q26 results. 
Previously, market expected limited unit profitability improvement in 2026, on top 
of market share gains. Now management is more optimistic towards full-year unit 
profit improvement. A positive feedback loop has resumed - market share gains, 
followed by better pricing power and more efficient cost structure vs. peers, leading 
to better-than-expected unit profitability and earnings growth.  
Share price correction brings a good entry point: ZTO's dual listed H shares 
(2057.HK, not covered) fell 3.3% on May 20 (vs. HSI -0.6%), after ZTO announced 
the resignation of a non-executive director from Alibaba (BABA.N, covered by Gary 
Yu) along with results, triggering market expectations of a  potential Alibaba stake 
reduction  (9% stakes as of end-25). ZTO said it couldn't rule out this possibility.  We 
would view any pullback in the US listed stock as a good entry point given no 
fundamental concerns behind  the share price correction, and we expect ZTO to 
resume share repurchases soon. We estimate ZTO's share repurchases may reach US
$0.8bn in the next 12 months (plus YTD US$0.6bn), indicating ~8% shareholder 
return on the last close.
Earnings and PT adjustments: We raise our adj. net profit forecast by 5.4% and 
5.3% for 2026 and 2027, respectively, following slightly higher unit profit 
assumptions. Our forecasts now imply 21% and 16% YoY growth in adj. net profits in 
2026 and 2027, respectively. Our 2026 earnings are 8.5% above consensus. Our 
end-2026 price target rises 5.6% to US$30.1, implying ~14x 2026e P/E. 
Robust 2Q26 profit growth outlook, reiterate OW: We expect ~10% YoY volume 
growth in 2Q26, and a moderate QoQ improvement in unit profitability amid 
seasonality.  We expect ~Rmb2.9bn adj. NP for 2Q26, a ~40% YoY increase from a 
low base. We think ZTO's current valuation at 11x P/E reflects the market's lack of 
confidence in its sustained profit growth given competition uncertainties. However, 
we think  earnings upward revisions and valuation expansion will likely continue, 
supporting our optimistic view on its risk-reward.

1Q26 Results and Call Takeaways
Results summary
1Q26 non-GAAP net income: Rmb2,377mn, +5.2% YoY, largely in-line with MSe 
(Rmb2,347mn,+4%). 
• 1Q26 parcel revenue grew 22.5% YoY to Rmb13.1bn, driven by 13.2% YoY volume 
growth, core ASP +8.2% YoY (peers: -2~+14% YoY). Retail parcel volume growth 
continues to outpace in the mix, contributing to Rmb18 cent KA-related ASP 
increase, partly offset by Rmb9 cents (-11 cents in 4Q25 and -14 cents in 3Q25) of 
vol incentives.
• Core express unit cost was up Rmb8 cents YoY: KA costs led to 15-cent hike, offset 
by 6-cent efficiency gains.
• GP and  OP were up 20.3% (MSe: +18% YoY) and 5.8% (+22% recurring) YoY, 
respectively. Unit GP was Rmb0.33, +6.3% YoY. 
• Implied unit  net profit was Rmb0.25, -7% YoY. Smaller government grants in 1Q26 
amid recognition timing difference, we think. (MSe: Rmb0.24, down Rmb0.02 
QoQ; peers: Rmb0.07-0.18, -Rmb0.02~+Rmb0.01 QoQ).
- Market share in 1Q26 was 20.3%, +1.3ppt YoY. 
- OCF was Rmb2.8bn in 1Q26, vs. Rmb2.4bn in 1Q25. Capex was Rmb1.8bn in 1Q26, -8% 
YoY.
- 2026 outlook unchanged: parcel volume 42.4-43.5bn  (MSe: 43.1bn), up 10-13%  YoY.

Earnings Estimate and Price Target Revisions
We raise our EPS forecasts for 2026-27e by 5% and 5%, respectively, reflecting better-
than-expected unit cost improvement YTD2026 and healthy ASP amid anti-involution. We 
also introduce our 2028e EPS of Rmb18.7, implying 14% YoY adj. net profit growth in 
2028.
Our WACC assumption remains unchanged at 13.2%. With unchanged  probability 
weightings for bull/base/bear cases, our probability-weighted price target is lifted by 6% 
to US$30.1 from US$28.5 previously, largely in line with our earnings changes. Our price 
target implies 14x 2026e P/E, slightly higher vs. the domestic peer average of 13x given 
market leadership.
BULL CASE: 15% WEIGHTING (unchanged)
Scenario value: US$39.60 (US$37.50 previously)
Revenue growth of 18%, 17% and 14% for 2026e, 2027e and 2028e, respectively, and 
adjusted EBIT margins of 26%, 27% and 27%: We assume the express industry 
consolidates faster than expected, and ZTO takes significantly more market share than 
peers.
BASE CASE: 75% WEIGHTING (unchanged)
Scenario value: US$30.0 (US$28.4 previously)
Revenue growth of 16%, 15% and 11% for 2026e, 2027e and 2028e, respectively, and 
adjusted EBIT margins of 24%, 25% and 25%: We expect industry volumes to grow 8%, 9% 
and 9% YoY in 2026-28e. We also expect ZTO's market share to rise 0.7ppt in 2026e, 
0.6ppt in 2027e, and 0.3ppt in 2028e given its more balanced network partner interests, 
better cost control, and better service quality.
BEAR CASE: 10% WEIGHTING (unchanged)
Scenario value: US$16.80 (US$15.70 previously)
Revenue growth of 13%, 12% and 8% for 2026e, 2027e and 2028e, respectively, and 
adjusted EBIT margins of 16%, 17% and 18%: We assume lower volume growth due to 
weaker-than-expected e-commerce demand, and lower-than-expected adjusted EBIT 
margins for 2026-28e, impaired by cost inflation in serving key accounts, as well as 
declining ASPs due to intensified price competition.
