Q: How did the results compare vs expectations?
A: NONB released 2025 full year and Q126 results. 2025 revenue, +8.0% YoY, and NPAT, 
+8.1% YoY, were in line with preliminary results. We highlight that BONB announced a 
final DPS of Rmb0.9 for 2025, rendering full year DPS of Rmb1.2. This implies 3.7% 
dividend yield, based on latest closing, and 27.0% payout ratio in 2025, a sizable step 
up from 21.9% in 2024. While BONB's dividend yield is not the most attractive among 
peers, the higher payout ratio should be appreciated by investors.
In 1Q26, both revenue and NPAT YoY growth recorded acceleration from 2025, ahead 
of our and market expectation. Revenue grew by 10.2% YoY (vs +8.0% YoY in 2025), 
driven by +14.4% YoY in NII, +81.7% YoY in net fee income, despite a 26.4% YoY 
decline in other non-NII income. NII was supported by a sequentially higher NIM at 
1.73% (+5bps QoQ/-7bps YoY, UBSSe), and a robust AIEA growth of 19.1% YoY. The 
strong growth in net fee income could be due to larger volume in financial products 
distribution, amid deposit migration. PPOP grew by 15.3% YoY in Q1, as  operating 
expenses declined by 1.7% YoY. Q126 NPAT rose by 10.3% YoY (vs +8.1% YoY in 
2025), despite the 21.6%% YoY higher allowance for impairment.
Q: What were the most noteworthy areas in the results?
A: (1) Q126 NIM sat at 1.73%, +5/-7bps QoQ/YoY, in line with industry trend. We 
calculate  asset yield reached 3.18% in Q1, -4/-48bps QoQ/YoY. As such, we expect 
deposit cost continued its rapid downward trend (-21/-42bps HoH/YoY in H225). In 
H225, we estimate -28/-20bps yield decline HoH for loans/investments. 
(2) Asset quality was steady. NPL ratio was unchanged QoQ/YoY at 0.76%. NPL 
formation in Q126 stood at 78bps (annualized), we calculate,  similar to the 70-85bps 
range since Q225.  Provision coverage ratio edged down slightly, at 369.4%, down 
3.4ppt QoQ. SML ratio reached 1.06% by end of Q126, -8bps/+6bps QoQ/YoY.
(3) Total loans grew by 15.7% YoY as of end-Q126, slightly decelerating from +17.4% 
YoY by end-2025, as expected. QoQ, total loans grew by Rmb163.7bn in Q126, flat vs in 
Q125. Corporate loan balance recorded 29.9% YoY increase (vs +30.5% YoY by end-
2025), while retail loans declined by 3.2% YoY (vs -4.2% YoY by end-2025). Deposit 
grew at a notably slower pace, +2.9% YoY by end-Q1.
Q: How would we expect investors to react?
A: We expect a  positive reaction on the clean beat.  BONB is trading at 0.85x 2026E P/B.

Company Description
Bank of Ningbo (BONB) was established in 1997 and was the first city commercial bank to list 
on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange. It operates mainly in Ningbo and other cities in Zhejiang 
province. As of end-2024, it had Rmb3.1trn in total assets with 26,976 employees and 486 
branches.
Valuation Method and Risk Statement
We derive our price target of BONB based on the P/BV versus ROE valuation method.
We think the major upside risks for Bank of Ningbo are: 1) stronger-than-expected retail loan 
growth; and 2) further policy support on SMEs and consumptions.
Major downside risks for Bank of Ningbo are: 1) prolonged or deteriorating macro headwinds 
weighing on loan demand and quality in the medium to long term; 2) intensifying trade 
conflict; and 3) MTM fluctuations in financial investments.
