 Our Initial Thoughts on the NSI Acquisition 
HUBB announced the acquisition of NSI Industries 
This morning, Hubbell announced that it has entered into a definitive agreement 
to acquire NSI Industries, a leading provider of electrical fittings, connectors, 
components, 
and 
wire 
management 
products, 
from 
Sentinel 
Capital 
Partners. The total transaction value is $3bn in cash, which represents ~15.5x 
NSI's anticipated 2026 adj. EBITDA. The acquisition is expected to close in mid-
2026. In this report, we lay out our initial thoughts on the transaction. 
A brief introduction to NSI Industries 
NSI Industries is a leading manufacturer of electrical fittings, connectors, 
components, and wire management products, serving industrial, infrastructure, 
and commercial end markets. The company offers a comprehensive portfolio of 
>15k branded electrical products through >2k distributors in NA, with well-
respected brands including Bridgeport fittings, Polaris connectors, and Tork 
timers. NSI anticipates generating ~$570m revenue in 2026 and is characterized 
by strong organic growth that aligns with higher-growth segments within HUBB's 
Electrical segment, contributing to an attractive financial profile expected to be 
accretive to Hubbell and HES adj. operating margins/adj. EPS. 
The deal makes sense strategically… 
While Hubbell already possesses a comprehensive Electrical Solutions portfolio, 
the acquisition of NSI strategically enhances its offerings by integrating a 
complementary suite of products. This move is particularly timely as 
electrification is driving significant growth across the electrical products industry. 
NSI's established position in strategic growth verticals like light industrial, data 
center, and network infrastructure applications creates an opportunity for HUBB 
to strengthen its market penetration and increase its wallet share with core utility 
and electrical customers. We anticipate the combination will unlock cross-selling 
opportunities and channel conversions within the Electrical segment, and there 
could also be cost synergies – although these were not explicitly laid out in the 
press release. This acquisition clearly aligns with Hubbell's long-term objective to 
expand its critical infrastructure solutions offering, positioning it to capitalize 
more effectively on evolving industry demands.
