Laopu’s share price has fallen 22% in Apr-to-date (vs. HSI/MSCI China 
+5%/+5%), due mainly to investors’ worries about the retail sales trend 
amid gold price dynamics (down 14% vs. peak at end Jan) and potential 
placement. We remain positive on Laopu, given: 1) the resilient demand 
in the recent festive events; 2) the low possibility of share placement in 
the near term; and, 3) channel upgrades. We think the risk reward is 
attractive, with the current valuation (10.7x/8.7x 26E/27E P/E, Bloomberg 
consensus), and expect re-rating from here. We are confident in Laopu’s 
2026 (JPMe sales/earnings +58%/+71%), driven by: 1) strengthening 
brand equity; 2) new product pipelines; 3) channel upgrades & expansion; 
and 4) VIC management bearing fruit. We raise our 26-27E earnings 
estimates by 5-8% (given the resilient demand). Our DCF-based PT 
remains at HK$1,296, implying 19x 2027E P/E. We see Laopu as best 
positioned to benefit from experience-led growth with a systematic 
approach (disciplined store count, a direct to consumer model and 
differentiated service quality upheld by a highly selective/trained team). 
Laopu is our top pick in the China consumer sector.   
• Resilient demand amid gold price fluctuations. Our recent channel 
checks in Shanghai (>one fifth offline sales) suggested resilient 
consumer demand during festival events (started on 25 Apr). Key 
findings: 1) solid traffic: 10-30min line-up (stronger than peers); 2) 
robust conversion rate: c30 orders per hour (>70% conversion rate); 3) 
further penetration into new customers: c50% were new customers; 4) 
shortage of popular SKUs, such as the new Rose Window Necklace with  
Pink Sapphire (launched in Mar). We also noticed Laopu’s active 
initiatives to strengthen its sense of value to customers amid gold price 
dynamics, such as a lower threshold for gifts (e.g., 5g gold coin gifts for 
ticket size over Rmb250k currently, vs. Rmb500k prior). Laopu’s 
resilient trend vs. peers, demonstrates its strengthening brand equity and 
leading position in the heritage gold market.  
• Low possibility of a capital raise, near-term. We expect sufficient 
internal cash to support inventory preparation, near-term (calculations 
per Table 1). This should ease investors’ concern on a share placement 
in the near term (to recap, Laopu placed new shares 2 times in 2025, one 
in mid-May for new store openings and one at end Oct mainly for 
inventory reserves for the upcoming peak season, in our view). 
• Channel upgrades: We think Laopu is on track to upgrade channels to 
strengthen growth visibility in 2026: with 1) upgrades of 8-12 domestic 
stores (better locations and larger selling areas); and, 2) 4-6 new 
overseas stores (mainly in 2H26). Potential markets include Singapore, 
HK, Macau, Japan, Korea, Malaysia and Thailand.

Summary Investment Thesis and Valuation
Investment Thesis 
Founded in 2009, Laopu Gold is a niche China heritage gold 
brand and the only Chinese jewelry brand positioned in the 
luxury segment. While Laopu has only 45 boutiques (as of 
2025), it has 85%+ sales exposure to tier 1/new tier 1 cities, 
the highest single-store sales and a pricing premium vs other 
Chinese local brands. 
We expect Laopu to deliver a sales/NP CAGR of 36%/41% 
over 2025-28E, given: 1) a fast-growing China heritage gold 
segment; 2) store network expansion opportunities in both 
China and overseas; and 3) SSSG supported by enhanced 
brand equity, penetration based on innovation and a strong 
value proposition vs international brands, and an outstanding 
retail experience. 
Valuation
Our DCF-based Dec-26 PT of HK$1,296 implies 19x 27E 
P/E. We derive our 9.4% WACC assuming a 4.3% risk-free 
rate, 7% risk premium, 11.7% cost of equity and terminal 
growth rate of 3.0%.
