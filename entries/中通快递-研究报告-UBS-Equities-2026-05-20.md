Winning on quality over quantity  
Q1 slightly missed on lower subsidies; core profit tracked revenue
ZTO's Q126 results slightly missed expectations. Revenue grew strongly by 22% YoY (vs. 
consensus estimate of 18%), while adjusted net profit only recorded a 5% YoY increase 
(vs. expectation of 7%), dragged down by a reduction in government subsidies. 
Excluding this one-off impact, the YoY growth of the company's operating profit was 
largely consistent with that of revenue. In addition, the company maintains its full-year 
parcel volume growth guidance of 10-13%.
Market share gains driven by quality clients
Despite the industry's growth slowdown amid the "anti-involution" backdrop, ZTO’s 
parcel vol achieved a robust 13% YoY growth in Q1, notably outpacing the industry 
(6%) and expanding its market share by 1.4ppts, thanks to its better customer mix and 
service quality. Meanwhile, express ASP improved by Rmb11 cents YoY, driven by the 
growth of KA (Key Accounts) business (contributing an 18-cent lift) and increased parcel 
weight (a 2-cent lift), which more than offset the drag from volume-based incentives 
(dragging down ASP by 9 cents). On the cost side, Q1 unit cost increased by Rmb8 cents 
YoY, which was also mainly due to the expansion of KA vol. However, the core cost per 
parcel decreased by 6 cents YoY, with transport and sorting cost down 4 cents and 2 
cents, respectively, demonstrating the results of the company's cost-reduction and 
efficiency-enhancement initiatives (automation, AI, etc.). Overall, the company's 
adjusted NP per parcel in Q1 was Rmb25 cents (vs. 26 cents in last Q2).
Policy tailwinds support higher earnings forecast + buyback commitment
According to our channel checks, the industry's "anti-involution" policies are expected 
to continue. We further raise our assumption for the company's express ASP for the full 
year 2026, which in turn moves our forecast for its adjusted NP 4% higher. Looking at 
the second quarter, we expect the company's adjusted NP to achieve YoY growth of 
c40% on a low base last year. Regarding shareholder returns, ZTO's CFO, Ms. Yan, 
mentioned during our conference call today that the remaining US$800 million of the 
previously issued US$1.5 billion convertible bonds will largely be used for share 
buybacks within the year.
Valuation: Maintain Buy
We are optimistic that ZTO will further consolidate and expand its leading advantage 
against the backdrop of "anti-involution," and we maintain our Buy rating.

Company Description
ZTO Express is a leading express delivery company in China. It provides express delivery 
services via a nationwide network and other value-added logistics services. As of 31 Dec 
2019, the company had centralised control and management of 91 sorting hubs, a fleet of 
over 7,350 line-haul vehicles (6,450 self-owned) and a market share of 19% in China. ZTO 
Express was listed on the NYSE in 2016.
Valuation Method and Risk Statement
Our price target is based on PE. ZTO’s volume growth is highly sensitive to the pace of 
eCommerce and economic growth. A meaningful deceleration could drive downside to our 
EPS estimates and stock price. The other major downside risk is from a larger-than-expected 
decline in average price per parcel and smaller-than-expected cost savings from fleet 
management and automation. Other sources of error include, but are not limited to, 
significant changes in logistics strategy by large eCommerce companies, decline in domestic 
consumption, exchange rate volatility and US regulation changes which could impact ADR 
performances.
