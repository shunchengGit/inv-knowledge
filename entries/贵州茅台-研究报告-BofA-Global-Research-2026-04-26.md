Kweichow Moutai Co. Ltd. (A) 
1Q a mixed bag; strong i-Moutai with 
margin pressure set to ease ahead 
Reiterate Rating: BUY | PO: 1,630 CNY | Price: 1,458 CNY   
  
 
 
1Q a mixed bag; i-Moutai strength vs. margin erosion 
1Q was largely in-line, with revenue/NPAT +6.3%/+1.5%. Revenue resilience was owing 
to i-Moutai, though margins declined due to an unfavorable mix shift and lower ASPs of 
customized and series liquor. We broadly maintain estimates and reiterate Buy with PO 
of RMB1630 for three reasons. 1) Easier comps should emerge, as the industry will lap 
the anti-graft campaign from May and P&L cleanup from 2H. 2) We view Moutai as the 
most defensive proxy for a likely bottoming cycle, with better visibility on branding and 
channel reforms, which have driven a recovery in sell‑out and channel profitability & 
cash flow, alongside stabilization of channel prices. In contrast, other large-cap peers all 
face their own issues. 3) Moutai offers a 3.6% dividend yield, or c.4% total shareholder 
yield, including share buyback, which appears attractive in a low-interest-rate 
environment.   
2026E: sales +6%; margin pressure to ease ahead 
We expect FY26 sales to be +6%, with 2Q growth to be below 1Q on a low season, but 
2H to accelerate on a low base (revenue was +11%/+7%/flat/-19% in 1Q/2Q/3Q/4Q25). 
We expect margin erosion to ease ahead, due to 1) Moutai’s recent Feitian price hike, 2) 
April rollout of consignment sales for customized liquor and 3) easy comps by 2H. 
1Q26 result highlights: decoding the mix change 
1) Moutai liquor’s sales were +5.6%. We estimate Feitian to be +70% and customized 
liquor to be -60-70%. 2) Series liquor sales were +12.2%. 3) Customer advance payment 
was -66% YoY/-62% QoQ, due to a mix shift to DTC sales. 4) By channel, DTC sales were 
+27% YoY (55% of sales), 73% of which was i-Moutai (+267% to RMB21.55bn). We 
estimate 75-80% of i-Moutai 1Q sales to be Feitian. Wholesale was -11% YoY, partly on 
lower shipment to SOE distributors, per channel checks. 5) Gross margin was -331bp to 
74.9%, as we estimate customized liquor (with higher margins) dropped to <20% of 
Moutai liquor sales (vs. c.50% in 2025) after recent channel reforms. S&D/sales was 
largely flat at 2.9%, and G&A/sales was -29bp to 3.5%. As a result, operating margin was 
-338bp to 68.6%.

Company Description 
With over 800 years of history, Moutai is the largest baijiu 
company in China, with over 50% volume share in 
premium-end baijiu. Its core brand, Moutai (including 53-, 
43- and 38-proof), contributes 91% of revenue. The 
remaining sales come from other series, represented by 
Moutai Prince, Ying Bin and Lai Mou. It is the most well-
known baijiu brand globally and is positioned at the 
premium end of the market with the highest ASP and gross 
margin. The firm was listed in SHEx in August 27, 2001. 
Investment Rationale 
We rate Moutai Buy. The company enjoys indisputable 
industry leadership, brand strength and scarcity value, 
which have led to much more EPS buffers, a smoother 
performance cycle amid the downturn and healthier 
channel dynamics.
