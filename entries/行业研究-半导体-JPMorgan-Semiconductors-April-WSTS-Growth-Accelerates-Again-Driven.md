WSTS semiconductor industry data was published for the month of April 2026. 
After an 88% Y/Y increase in industry sales in March 2026 (up 25% Y/Y ex. 
memory), April industry sales growth accelerated to +106% Y/Y (up 33% Y/Y 
ex.memory). On an M/M basis, after a 14% M/M increase in sales in March(+12% 
M/M ex. memory), April industry sales declined 2.2% M/M (-0.7% M/M ex. 
memory). Overall units (ex-discretes) declined 4% M/M (+24% Y/Y), while 
pricing climbed 1.8% M/M (and was up 70% Y/Y – a step-up relative to +62% Y/Y 
in March). Memory pricing improved materially again in April, with DRAM up 
55% M/M and flash up 41% M/M, while analog was essentially flat (below 
historical +LSD% M/M trends) and MCU up ~2% M/M (~better than typical -
LSD-MSD%). Unit shipment growth of -2% M/M was ahead of seasonal trends, 
driven by better-than-typical sequential performance in analog, MCU, MPU and 
flash, but offset by moderately below-seasonal growth in DRAM. On a trailing 
3-month basis, semis revenue grew +29% Y/Y  – a slight uptick from +28% Y/Y 
in March. With significant momentum coming out of Q1, even if we were to 
assume Q2/Q3/Q4 growth rates in line with historical seasonal trends (highly 
conservative, in our view), full-year industry sales would be up ~80% Y/Y, and in 
the range of $1.4-1.5T (representing 5/10-yr CAGRs of ~20%/~16% for the 
industry). Overall, growth trends are consistent with post-Q1 commentary from 
semis companies under our coverage, indicating further uplift in demand 
momentum, with bookings, backlog, and customer escalations continuing to build 
in the weeks since the companies’ Q1 prints (as we highlighted in our TMC 
conference takeaways report). Against this backdrop, we see continued support for 
our 2026 sector/stock outlook thesis that calls for outsized growth in industry 
revenue, underpinned by continued momentum in AI spending that broadly 
benefits the semis value chain, with providers of accelerated compute, memory and 
networking being most favorably levered to incremental growth in spending. We 
also continue to anticipate improvements in cyclical trends across most end 
markets, aided by a much healthier customer and channel inventory environment, 
though we remain cognizant of potential negative impacts of elevated memory 
pricing and supply shortages on demand within consumer/client-levered end 
markets (see our global team’s latest memory update here).
See page 6 for analyst certification and important disclosures.
